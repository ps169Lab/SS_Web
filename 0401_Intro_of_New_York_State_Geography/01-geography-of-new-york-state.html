Geography of New York State

Discovering What Makes New York State Special

Vocabulary Words

  • Geography - the study of Earth's land, water, and climate
  • Diverse - having many different types or kinds
  • Climate - the usual weather patterns in a place
  • Hemisphere - half of the Earth

Where is New York State?

New York State is located in the northeastern region of the United States, positioned on the continent of North America. The state is often called "New York State" to distinguish it from New York City, which represents just one part of this diverse state.


Geography is the study of Earth's land, water, and how they affect life. New York State has so many different kinds of places - mountains, forests, valleys, lakes, rivers, and even ocean coastline! All these different landscapes create special homes for many types of plants and animals.

New York's Strategic Location

New York State borders the Atlantic Ocean to the southeast and two Great Lakes - Lake Erie and Lake Ontario - to the west and northwest. This unique position makes New York the only state that borders both the Great Lakes and the Atlantic Ocean, creating exceptional opportunities for trade and transportation.


Diverse Landscapes

Contrary to popular belief, most of New York State consists of rural countryside rather than urban areas. The state contains two major mountain ranges: the Adirondack Mountains in the north and the Catskill Mountains in the southeast. These mountainous regions provide habitats for wildlife and recreational opportunities.


New York is famous for its numerous lakes, particularly the Finger Lakes region. These eleven long, narrow lakes were carved by glaciers during the last ice age and resemble fingers when viewed on a map. The glacial activity also created many of the state's other distinctive landforms.


Climate Patterns

New York State has four very different seasons - spring, summer, fall, and winter. Because the state is so big and has so many different kinds of land, the weather can be very different in different places. Areas near the Great Lakes get lots and lots of snow, while places near the ocean stay a bit warmer.

Fall is a super popular time for visitors who come for "leaf peeping" - that means looking at all the beautiful colors when the leaves change from green to red, orange, and yellow! This amazing color show happens when trees get ready to sleep through the cold winter months.

Geographic Significance

Learning about New York's geography helps us understand how the land and water affect where people choose to live and work. The state's many different landscapes have shaped its history, from where Native Americans first lived to where our modern cities are today. It shows us how the land and people work together!
