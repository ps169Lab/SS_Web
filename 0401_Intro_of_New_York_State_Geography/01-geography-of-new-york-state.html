Where is New York?

<<<<<<< HEAD New York is in the northeast part of the United States. It's on the continent called North America. We say "New York State" because there's also a "New York City" in the state.


Geography is learning about the Earth, like the land and water. New York has many kinds of land and water. There are mountains, forests, lakes, rivers, and the ocean! These places are homes for plants and animals.

New York is Special

New York has the ocean on one side and big lakes on the other. It's the only state with both! This makes it a good place for people to live and trade.


Different Lands

Most of New York is nature, not cities. There are two big mountains: the Adirondack Mountains and the Catskill Mountains. Animals live in these mountains.


All states have different types of land and water that make them special. New York State has many different kinds of places - mountains, forests, lakes, rivers, and ocean coastline. This mix of land and water creates different areas where different plants and animals can live.

New York's Location

New York State touches the Atlantic Ocean on one side and two of the Great Lakes on the other side. The Great Lakes are Lake Erie and Lake Ontario. New York is the only state that touches both the Great Lakes and the Atlantic Ocean. This makes it very special for trade and travel.


Different Types of Land

Most people think New York is just a big, busy city. But most of New York State is actually countryside with mountains, forests, and lakes. The state has two main mountain areas called the Catskills and the Adirondacks.


New York also has many beautiful lakes. The Finger Lakes got their name because they look like long, thin fingers on a map. These lakes were made long ago by huge sheets of ice called glaciers.


Weather and Climate

New York State has four seasons with cold winters and warm summers. Different parts of the state have different weather. The areas near the Great Lakes get lots of snow in winter. The areas near the ocean are a little warmer.

Fall is a favorite time for many people to visit New York because the leaves on the trees turn beautiful colors like red, orange, and yellow. This happens because the weather gets cooler and the trees get ready for winter.

Why Geography Matters

Learning about New York's geography helps us understand how the land, water, and weather work together. It shows us why certain plants and animals live in different places, and how people have used the land for farming, building cities, and transportation throughout history.
