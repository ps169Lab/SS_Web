New York's Animals

Animals have special things that help them live in their homes. New York has many places for animals to live, like forests and oceans. The animals have special things to help them!


Animals have fur to keep warm in winter. Birds have feathers to fly and stay warm. Fish have gills to breathe underwater. Plants have roots to drink water and leaves to make food from the sun!

How Animals Live in Winter

New York's winters are cold. Animals need special ways to live when it's freezing and there's not much food.


The beaver is New York's animal! Beavers have thick fur to stay warm in the water. Black bears sleep in the winter. They eat a lot in the fall to get ready for their long sleep!

How Animals Find Food

Animals have special ways to find and eat food. They have different body parts and ways to hunt.


The bluebird is New York's bird! Bluebirds have good eyes to see bugs and a beak to catch them. Brook trout have colors that help them hide in the water. They eat small bugs.

Hiding and Staying Safe

Animals hide from animals that want to eat them. They use colors and patterns to blend in or look scary.


The weasel changes colors! It turns white in winter to hide in the snow and brown in summer to match the dirt.

What Plants Do

New York's plants have ways to live in different weather. Trees sleep in the winter and grow in the spring and summer!


Some plants eat meat! Pitcher plants live where the soil is bad. They catch bugs to eat.

Saving Animals

New York has parks to keep animals safe. This helps animals and plants live happily. Kids can learn about New York's nature!
