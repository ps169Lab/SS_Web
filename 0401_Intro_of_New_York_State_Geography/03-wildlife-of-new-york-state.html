Wildlife of New York State

Discovering the Amazing Plants and Animals That Call New York Home

Vocabulary Words

  • Adaptation - special features that help animals survive in their environment
  • Native - plants and animals that naturally belong in a place
  • Habitat - the natural home where plants and animals live
  • Ecosystem - all the living and non-living things in an area working together

New York's Diverse Wildlife

Adaptations are special features that help animals survive in their homes. New York State has so many different places to live, from mountain forests to ocean shores, and the animals have amazing special features to help them live in these different places!


All mammals have fur or hair to keep them warm during New York's cold winters. Birds use their feathers to fly and stay warm too. Fish and other water animals have gills to breathe underwater, while plants have roots to drink up water and nutrients from the soil, and leaves to make their own food from sunlight!

Winter Survival Strategies

New York's cold winters mean animals need special ways to survive when it's freezing and there's not much food around. These special ways include changes to their bodies, different behaviors, and even changing how their bodies work inside!


The American Beaver is New York's official state animal! Beavers are amazing at living in cold water because they have thick, waterproof fur and special blood flow that keeps them warm. Black bears have a different trick - they sleep most of the winter and live off the fat they stored up by eating lots of food in the fall!

Foraging and Feeding Adaptations

Different animals have special ways of finding and eating food based on where they live and what they eat. These special features include different body parts, super-powered senses, and special ways of hunting or looking for food.


The Eastern Bluebird is New York's state bird! These beautiful birds have super sharp eyesight to spot tiny insects and a pointed beak that's perfect for catching them. Brook Trout have special colors and patterns that help them blend in with the rocks and plants in streams, so they can hide from bigger fish while they hunt for small water bugs to eat.

Camouflage and Defense

Many animals use camouflage as their main way to hide from predators (animals that want to eat them). This means they have colors, patterns, or behaviors that help them blend in with their surroundings or look scary to animals that might want to hurt them.


The Short-tailed Weasel is like a furry chameleon! It changes colors with the seasons - turning white in winter to blend in with the snow and brown in summer to match the dirt and plants. This color-changing trick, called molting, helps them stay hidden all year long!

Plant Adaptations

New York's plants have special ways to deal with changing seasons and different types of soil. Trees like the Sugar Maple have figured out how to sleep through the winter and then grow really well when spring and summer come back!


Some plants in New York are meat-eaters! Plants like pitcher plants live in swampy areas where the soil doesn't have much food for them, so they've developed special traps to catch and eat insects. It's like having a snack to make up for the poor soil!

Conservation Efforts

New York State has set aside huge protected areas like Adirondack and Catskill Parks to keep wildlife habitats safe. These conservation efforts make sure that native animals and plants can keep living happily, and that kids in the future will be able to learn about and enjoy New York's amazing nature too!
