What New York Looks Like

Let's See New York's Land and Nature!

New Words

  • Land - ground, like hills and valleys
  • Place - a big area with the same weather and plants
  • Ice - frozen water
  • River - water that flows to the ocean

How New York's Land Was Made

Land is what the Earth looks like, like mountains and rivers. New York has many different land shapes. They were made a long, long time ago by ice and other things.


A long time ago, big sheets of ice called glaciers moved across New York. When the ice melted, it left behind the Hudson River valley, the Finger Lakes, and rocks and dirt.

Mountain Areas

New York has two big mountain areas. They change the weather and help plants and animals live there.


The Adirondack Mountains are in the north. The highest mountain is Mount Marcy. The Catskill Mountains are older and have waterfalls.


Water Ways

New York has many rivers and lakes. They give us water and help us travel. The ice made these rivers and lakes a long time ago.


The Hudson River goes a long way to the ocean. The Finger Lakes are long lakes in the middle of New York. They help the weather and help farmers grow things.


New York touches two Great Lakes. These lakes have lots of fresh water! The Niagara River connects the lakes and makes Niagara Falls. The water falls down a long way!

Flat Lands

New York has flat lands near the ocean and lakes. These lands are good for farms and cities.


Long Island is flat land made by ice. The flat lands near Buffalo used to be lakes! These places are good for growing food and building.

How It Helps

New York's land shapes the weather and homes for plants and animals. Mountains are cool and wet. Flat lands are good for farms and cities. The land helps plants, animals, and people!
