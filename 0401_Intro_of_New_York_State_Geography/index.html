Introduction to New York State Geography

Discovering the Empire State's Diverse Landscapes and Communities

Welcome to New York State!

Welcome to your exploration of New York State! Often called the "Empire State," New York is much more than just the famous city that shares its name. New York State has a diverse geography that represents and examines the climate and biomes found throughout our region and the world.

What You'll Discover

In this unit, you'll explore five important aspects of New York State geography that will help you understand what makes this state so special:

  • Geography of New York State - Learn about New York's location in the world and what makes it special
  • Physical Features of New York State - Discover the major landforms, mountains, rivers, and lakes that shape our state
  • Wildlife of New York State - Explore the amazing plants and animals that have adapted to live in New York's diverse environments
  • Political Features of New York State - Understand how the state is organized into counties and cities, and how natural resources affect where people live
  • New York City - Learn about America's largest city and discover what makes each of its five boroughs unique

New York's Amazing Diversity

While many people think of New York as just a big, busy city, the truth is that most of New York State is made up of mountains, forests, valleys, lakes, and farmland. From the towering peaks of the Adirondack Mountains to the sandy beaches of Long Island, from the rushing waters of Niagara Falls to the bustling streets of New York City, our state offers incredible variety.

New York State is the only state that borders both the Great Lakes and the Atlantic Ocean, giving it unique advantages for trade, transportation, and recreation. This special location has helped make New York one of the most important states in America.
