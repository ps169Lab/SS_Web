How New York Works

Let's Learn About New York's Government!

New Words

  • Area - a part of a state
  • Capital - the city where the government is
  • People - how many people live somewhere
  • Big City - a city and towns around it

How New York is Set Up

New York is set up in areas with governments. These areas are called counties, cities, towns, and villages. They all help New York work.


New York has 62 counties. They are like big neighborhoods with their own governments. They help with police, schools, and roads.

Albany is the Capital

Albany is New York's capital city. The government is there. It's in the middle of the state and easy to get to.


Albany doesn't have many people, but it's important. It's been the capital for a long time!

Cities and Country

New York has cities and country areas. Cities have many people close together. Country areas have fewer people far apart.

New York City has the most people in the United States! Other big cities are Buffalo, Rochester, and Syracuse. These cities are good for trading and making things.

Where People Live

Most people in New York live in cities. New York City has the most.


The mountains don't have many people because it's hard to live there. Areas near rivers have more people.

How Land Helps People

The land and water help people choose where to live in New York. Rivers, lakes, and mountains help decide where cities are built.

The Hudson River helps people build towns. Buffalo is near the Great Lakes, so it's good for shipping.

Working Together

New York's areas work together. Cities, farms, and parks all help each other.


Learning how New York works shows us how the land, history, and government help the state.
