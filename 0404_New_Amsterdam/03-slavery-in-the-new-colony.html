Slavery in the New Colony

A Sad and Wrong Part of Early American History

Expert Vocabulary

  • Slavery: A cruel system where people are forced to work without pay and treated as property.
  • Labor: Hard physical work.
  • Injustice: Something that is unfair and morally wrong.
  • Essential: Absolutely necessary; something a system cannot function without.

The Arrival of Enslaved People

We have learned that the Dutch West India Company was a business looking for profit. Because they wanted to build a colony quickly and cheaply, they turned to the terrible practice of slavery. The first enslaved Africans were brought to New Amsterdam in 1626, only one year after the Dutch built the fort. These men and women did not choose to come here; they were stolen from their homes in Africa and forced to work for the company’s success. The Dutch West India Company wanted to make money. To do this, they brought enslaved people from Africa to the colony. These people were forced to move and work for no pay. This was very unfair and wrong, but the company used them to build the new town.

What Work Did Enslaved People Do?

The labor of enslaved people was essential to building New York. They did the hardest jobs that the merchants and leaders didn't want to do. Enslaved people cleared the thick forests, built the stone walls for Fort Amsterdam, and paved the muddy streets with cobblestones. On farms, they grew the wheat and corn that fed the colony. At the harbor, they loaded heavy bundles of furs onto ships heading back to Europe. Without their forced work, the colony would not have survived. Enslaved people did the hardest work in the colony. They cut down trees to clear land. They built the fort and the roads. They worked on farms to grow food like corn. They also carried heavy furs to the ships in the harbor. Their hard work helped the colony grow.

Summary: Forced Labor in New Amsterdam

Type of Work Specific Jobs How it Helped the Colony
Construction Building forts, houses, and stone walls. It gave the settlers a safe place to live and work.
Agriculture Planting and harvesting wheat and corn. It provided the food needed to feed the growing population.
Shipping Loading and unloading trade ships at the port. It allowed the Company to send furs to Europe for profit.
Skilled Trades Blacksmithing, cooking, and carpentry. It provided the tools and services the city needed to run.

Life and the Struggle for Freedom

Life was incredibly difficult for enslaved people. They were often treated as objects rather than human beings, and families could be separated at any time. Despite this injustice, they worked hard to keep their cultures and families together. Under Dutch rule, some were eventually able to earn "half-freedom," which allowed them to own a small piece of land, but they still had to work for the Company whenever they were called. Even in these terrible conditions, enslaved people never stopped hoping and fighting for their right to be free. Life was very hard for enslaved people. They were treated poorly and often separated from their families. But they did not give up. They tried to keep their families together. Some even got a little bit of land to farm, but they were still not fully free. They always hoped for a better life.
