Important People of New Amsterdam

The Leaders Who Shaped New York's History

Expert Vocabulary

  • Director-General: The title for the main leader or governor of the colony.
  • Patroon: A wealthy landowner who was given a large estate in exchange for bringing settlers.
  • Treaty: A formal written agreement between two groups, often to end a war.
  • Petition: A formal request made by citizens to their leaders for a change.

Peter Minuit: The Founder

Peter Minuit was the first Director-General. He is most famous for the "purchase" of Manhattan in 1626. Minuit understood geography; he moved the main settlement from Fort Orange to Manhattan because the island had a deep-water harbor that didn't freeze as easily. He also started the Patroon system, which gave huge pieces of land to rich friends of the Company. While this brought more people to the colony, it also gave a few people way too much power. Peter Minuit was the first leader. He moved the colony to Manhattan Island. He chose Manhattan because it was a great place for ships to land. He also gave big pieces of land to rich people called Patroons so they would bring more families to live in the colony.

Willem Kieft and the War

Willem Kieft was a leader who struggled to get along with others. He tried to force the Native Americans to pay a tax to the Dutch, which caused a lot of anger. This led to a terrible conflict called "Kieft’s War." The fighting lasted for two years and was so bad that settlers had to hide inside the fort for safety. Eventually, a treaty was signed, but the damage was done—the Native Americans no longer trusted the Dutch leaders. Willem Kieft was a leader who was not very kind. He tried to make the Native Americans pay him money. This started a war that lasted for two years. It was a scary time, and people had to hide in the fort. After the war, a treaty was signed to stop the fighting.

Adriaen van der Donck: The People's Lawyer

Not all leaders were governors. Adriaen van der Donck was a lawyer who believed the colonists should have more of a say in how the town was run. He was upset with how Kieft handled the war and organized a petition to the Dutch West India Company to have him removed. Van der Donck wrote the first book describing the beauty of the Hudson Valley, helping people in Europe imagine what life was like in the New World. Adriaen van der Donck was a lawyer. He thought the leaders should listen to the people. He helped get the bad governor removed. He also wrote a book about how beautiful New York was so that people in Europe would want to move there.

Peter Stuyvesant: The Strict Governor

Peter Stuyvesant was the last and most famous Director-General. He was a very strict leader with a wooden leg. He cleaned up the muddy streets, built the first pier for ships, and created a protective wall (where Wall Street is today). However, he was very stubborn and didn't like diversity in religion. Because he didn't listen to the people, when the English ships arrived to take over in 1664, the colonists refused to fight for him. Peter Stuyvesant was the last Dutch leader. He was very strict and had a wooden leg. He fixed the roads and built a big wall to protect the city. But he was so bossy that the people got tired of him. When the English came to take the city, the people chose not to fight.

Class Discussion Questions

  • Choosing a Leader: If you were a settler in New Amsterdam, would you rather have a leader like Van der Donck (who listens) or Stuyvesant (who gets things fixed)? Why?
  • The Wall: Why do you think we still call the street in Manhattan "Wall Street" today? How does that connect to Peter Stuyvesant?
  • Geography Matters: Why was it a smart move for Peter Minuit to move the city from Albany (Fort Orange) down to Manhattan?
