New Amsterdam: The Busy Dutch Port

From Small Missions to a Global Business

Expert Vocabulary

  • Profit: The money a business makes after paying for its costs.
  • Diversity: Having many different types of people or languages in one place.
  • Architecture: The way buildings are designed and built.

Building on What We Know

Before New Amsterdam was a city, we learned it was just a small group of missions and trading posts. These early spots were built for two reasons: to trade for furs and to share religious beliefs. But by the 1600s, a powerful business called the Dutch West India Company took over. They realized that to make a real profit, they needed more than just camps—they needed a permanent colony with families, farms, and big ports. At first, the Dutch had small camps called missions and trading posts. They were only there to trade furs. Later, a big business called the Dutch West India Company took charge. They wanted to make more money, so they built a real city with homes and many people.

What did New Amsterdam Look Like?

Early New Amsterdam looked like a "little Netherlands." To manage the marshy land of Manhattan, the Dutch dug canals and built wooden windmills along the coast to grind grain. The architecture was unique: houses were tall and narrow with "stepped" brick roofs in bright colors. The streets were often muddy and narrow, filled with the sounds of horse-drawn carts and the salty smell of the harbor. It was a crowded town built entirely for business. The Dutch built the town to look like their old homes in Europe. They built tall brick houses and big windmills. The town had narrow, muddy streets and sat right next to the ocean harbor. It was a busy place for ships and sailors.

A Melting Pot of People

Because the Dutch West India Company focused on trade rather than religion, they welcomed almost anyone who could help the company make money. This created incredible diversity. In the streets of New Amsterdam, you would hear over 18 different languages being spoken! You might see Dutch merchants, French farmers, and even people from Brazil or Africa. This made the colony a "melting pot" where different cultures lived together to work and trade. The company wanted workers, so they let people from many different countries move to the colony. People in the streets spoke 18 different languages! It was a place where many different cultures lived and worked side-by-side.

Class Discussion Questions

  • The Company's Goal: How did the Dutch West India Company change the colony from the early "mission" days? What was their main focus?
  • Language and Business: If people in the town spoke 18 different languages, how do you think they were able to buy and sell things to each other?
  • Seeing the Past: Look at the "stepped" roofs of the Dutch houses. Why do you think the Dutch wanted their new homes to look exactly like their old homes in the Netherlands?
