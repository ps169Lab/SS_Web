THE DRAFTING OF THE CONSTITUTION

VOCABULARY

Framers: The people who wrote the Constitution.

Constitutional Convention: The meeting in Philadelphia where the Constitution was written.

Compromise: When people give up some of what they want to reach an agreement.

Ratify: To approve or accept officially.

THE CONSTITUTIONAL CONVENTION

In the summer of 1787, a special meeting called the Constitutional Convention took place in Philadelphia. Fifty-five men from twelve states came together to create a new plan for America's government. They met in Independence Hall - the same building where the Declaration of Independence was signed. In 1787, 55 men from 12 states met in Philadelphia to write a new plan for America's government. They met in the same building where the Declaration of Independence was signed.

THE PEOPLE WHO WROTE THE CONSTITUTION

The men who wrote the Constitution were called the Framers. Some important Framers were: The men who wrote the Constitution were called the Framers. Some important ones were:

  • George Washington from Virginia, who led the meeting
  • James Madison from Virginia, who took detailed notes and suggested many ideas
  • Benjamin Franklin from Pennsylvania, who was the oldest person there at 81 years old
  • Alexander Hamilton from New York, who wanted a strong national government

These men were lawyers, farmers, merchants, and leaders in their communities. They were well-educated and wanted to create a better government for the new nation. These men had different jobs like lawyers and farmers. They were smart and wanted to make a better government for America.


WHY THEY NEEDED A NEW PLAN

Before the Constitution, America had a different set of rules called the Articles of Confederation. This plan wasn't working well because: Before the Constitution, America had different rules called the Articles of Confederation. These rules weren't working well because:

  • The national government was too weak
  • It couldn't collect taxes to pay its bills
  • It couldn't solve problems between states.

DISAGREEMENTS ABOUT THE GOVERNMENT

The Framers didn't always agree on how the new government should work. They had big disagreements about: The Framers didn't always agree. They argued about:

Who Should Have More Power

  • Some people, like Alexander Hamilton, wanted a strong national government to solve big problems
  • Others, like George Mason, worried that a strong national government might take away states' rights and people's freedoms
  • This was one of their biggest fights! They argued about it almost every day

How Many Votes Should Each State Get?Big states (with more people) wanted more votes

  • Small states wanted every state to have the same number of votes
  • They were so mad about this that some almost went home!

They finally solved this problem with the Great Compromise:

    • They created two parts of Congress
    • In the House of Representatives, states with more people get more votes
    • In the Senate, every state gets exactly two votes, no matter how big or small


    The Problem of Slavery

    • Northern states wanted to count only free people for representation
    • Southern states wanted to count enslaved people too
    • They made the Three-Fifths Compromise, which counted each enslaved person as three-fifths of a person for representation (this unfair rule was later changed after the Civil War)

    FINISHING THE CONSTITUTION

    After four months of arguments and compromises, the Framers finished writing the Constitution on September 17, 1787. Thirty-nine of the Framers signed it. After four months of arguing and making deals, the Framers finished the Constitution on September 17, 1787. Thirty-nine men signed it.

    The Constitution created our system of government with three branches - legislative, executive, and judicial. It also created checks and balances to make sure no part of the government became too powerful. The Constitution created our government with three parts.
