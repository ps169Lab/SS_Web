Who Are the Hausa People?

The Hausa people are one of Nigeria's largest ethnic groups, with most living in northern Nigeria where the environment is drier and more like a savanna. The Hausa people have adapted their way of life to match their natural environment, which has less rainfall and more open grasslands than southern Nigeria. The Hausa people live in northern Nigeria. This area is dry with lots of grass and few trees. The Hausa people learned to live well in this dry place.

For hundreds of years, the Hausa people have been skilled traders and merchants. Their location in northern Nigeria, close to the Sahara Desert, made them important middlemen in trade between North Africa and the rest of West Africa. They used the natural resources of their environment to build a successful trading culture. The Hausa people are very good at trading. They live close to a big desert. This helped them trade with many different places. They use what they find in nature to make money.

Hausa Culture and Environment How Hausa People Live

The Hausa people live in an environment with less water and fewer trees than southern Nigeria, so they have developed cultural practices that work well in this drier climate. They are skilled at raising cattle, goats, and sheep that can survive in areas with less grass and water. The Hausa people live where there is not much water or trees. They learned to take care of cows, goats, and sheep that can live in dry places.

Hausa architecture reflects their environment too. They build houses with thick mud walls that stay cool during hot days and warm during cool nights. Many Hausa buildings have flat roofs and small windows to keep out the heat and dust from the dry northern winds. Hausa people build special houses. They use mud to make thick walls. These houses stay cool when it is hot and warm when it is cold. The houses have flat tops and small windows.

Important Hausa cities like Kano, Kaduna, and Zaria developed as trading centers where merchants could rest, trade goods, and resupply for long journeys. These cities were built near water sources and along important trade routes. Big Hausa cities like Kano grew up where traders could stop and rest. These cities were built near water and on roads that traders used.

The location of Hausa cities shows how geography affects where people choose to live.
