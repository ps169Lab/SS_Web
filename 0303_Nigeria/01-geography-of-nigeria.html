GEOGRAPHY OF NIGERIA

VOCABULARY

Continent: A really big piece of land with lots of countries on it.

Border: An invisible line that shows where one country stops and another starts.

Coastline: The place where land meets the ocean.

Delta: A place where a river splits into smaller streams as it flows into the ocean.

WHERE IS NIGERIA IN THE WORLD?

Nigeria is on the west side of Africa. It's just above the equator, which is an imaginary line around the middle of Earth. Nigeria is about as big as two Californias! It's the 32nd biggest country in the whole world. It sits between a dry desert up north and the Atlantic Ocean down south.


WHAT COUNTRIES BORDER NIGERIA?

Nigeria has four neighbor countries. Benin is on the west side. Niger is up north. Chad is to the northeast. Cameroon is to the east and shares the longest border with Nigeria. On the south side, Nigeria touches the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Guinea. Nigeria's coastline is 530 miles long - that's like driving from New York to Virginia! Nigeria touches four countries. WHERE IS NIGERIA IN THE WORLD?

Nigeria is in West Africa, the western part of the African continent. It acts like a bridge connecting the dry northern parts of Africa to the wet, tropical southern parts. IMPORTANT LAND AND WATER FEATURES

Nigeria has two major rivers - the Niger River and the Benue River. They meet in a Y-shape and flow all the way to the ocean. Nigeria actually got its name from the Niger River! The land is flat in the north and south, but the middle has hills and plateaus. The tallest mountain is called Chappal Waddi, and it's 2,419 meters high.
