GEOGRAPHY OF NIGERIA

VOCABULARY

Continent: A large area of land with many countries.

Border: The line where one country ends and another begins.

Coastline: The land next to the ocean.

Delta: Where a river meets the ocean and spreads out.

WHERE IS NIGERIA IN THE WORLD?

Nigeria is located in on the West side of the continent of Africa. It sits in the Northern Hemisphere, just north of the equator. Nigeria is about twice the size of California and is the 32nd largest country in the world. It is positioned between the dry desert to the north and the Atlantic Ocean to the south. 


WHAT COUNTRIES BORDER NIGERIA?

Nigeria shares borders with four neighboring countries. To the west is Benin. To the north is Niger. To the northeast is Chad. To the east is Cameroon, which has the longest border with Nigeria. To the south, Nigeria faces the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Guinea, giving it a coastline of 530 miles. Nigeria touches four countries. Benin is west. Niger is north. Chad is northeast. Cameroon is east. The Atlantic Ocean is south.

WHERE IS NIGERIA IN THE WORLD?

Nigeria is located in West Africa, which is the western part of the African continent. It sits between latitude lines 4° and 14° North and longitude lines 2° and 15° East. Nigeria is positioned as a bridge between the dry northern regions of Africa and the tropical southern regions. The country's location makes it an important part of West Africa. Nigeria is in West Africa. This is the west side of Africa. It connects the dry north part of Africa to the wet south part.


IMPORTANT LAND AND WATER FEATURES

Nigeria has important rivers and landforms. The Niger River and Benue River meet in a Y-shape and flow to the ocean. Nigeria gets its name from the Niger River. The land has plains in the north and south, with hills and plateaus in the center. The highest point is Chappal Waddi at 2,419 meters tall.
