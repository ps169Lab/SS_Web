NIGERIAN HOMES

VOCABULARY

Natural Resources: Materials from nature that people use to build things.

Biome: A large area with similar plants, animals, and weather.

Mbari: Special decorated houses built by the Igbo people.

Adobe: Mud bricks dried in the sun.

HOMES IN THE YORUBA REGION

The Yoruba people live in southwest Nigeria. It is hot and rainy there. They use mud, palm oil, and palm leaves to build homes. Their houses have thick mud walls. The walls keep the homes cool. There is a courtyard in the middle where families gather. The roofs are made from palm leaves. This keeps the rain out and lets air flow through. The Yoruba people live in southwest Nigeria. They use mud, palm oil, and palm leaves to build homes. Their houses have thick walls and courtyards in the middle.

HOMES IN THE HAUSA REGION

The Hausa people live in northern Nigeria. It is very dry there. They build homes with mud bricks dried in the sun. These are called adobe. There are few trees but lots of clay soil. Their houses have thick walls and small windows. Small windows keep out the hot sun and sand. Many homes have flat roofs and courtyards. Families stay cool in the shade. The Hausa people live in northern Nigeria where it is dry. They use mud bricks to build homes. Their houses have thick walls and small windows to stay cool.

HOMES IN THE IGBO REGION

The Igbo people live in southeast Nigeria. It is very wet and rainy there. They use wood, bamboo, and palm leaves to build homes. It rains a lot. So houses have steep roofs. Water runs off the roofs quickly. Some houses are built on stilts. Stilts keep them dry during floods. The Igbo also build special houses called Mbari. The Igbo people live in southeast Nigeria where there are forests and swamps. They use wood and palm leaves to build homes. Their houses have steep roofs for the rain.

THE SPECIAL PLACE OF MBARI HOMES

Mbari homes are very special in Nigerian culture. These are not houses where people live. Instead, they are art houses built by the Igbo people. They build them as gifts to their gods. Mbari houses are made from clay. They are decorated with colorful sculptures. The sculptures show people, animals, and spirits. The whole community works together for many years to build these houses. Mbari homes are special art houses. The Igbo people build them as gifts to their gods. They are made from clay and have colorful sculptures. The whole community helps build them.

HOW NATURAL RESOURCES SHAPE HOMES

Different parts of Nigeria have different materials. People use what they have nearby to build homes. In forests, people use wood and palm leaves. In dry areas, people use clay and mud. In swamps, people build on stilts. They use bamboo and reeds. The materials people use depend on where they live. People in different parts of Nigeria use different materials to build homes. Forest people use wood. Desert people use mud. Swamp people use bamboo and build on stilts.

HOMES REFLECT CULTURE

Nigerian homes show how culture comes from the environment. The materials people use come from nature around them. The way they build comes from nature. Even the decorations come from nature. Mbari houses are a good example. They use local clay. The sculptures show Igbo beliefs and stories. Nigerian homes show how people's culture comes from their environment.
