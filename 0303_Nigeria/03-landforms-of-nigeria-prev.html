NIGERIA'S IMPORTANT RIVERS

Nigeria has two major rivers. The Niger River is 2,547 miles long - like driving from New York to Los Angeles! The Benue River is its biggest branch. They meet in a Y-shape and flow to the Atlantic Ocean. Nigeria got its name from the Niger River! Nigeria has two big rivers - the Niger River and the Benue River. They meet and flow to the ocean. HOW RIVERS SHAPED NIGERIA'S HISTORY

For thousands of years, these rivers have been like water highways! People traveled on boats to visit towns and trade goods. Ancient kingdoms grew along the riverbanks because rivers provided water for drinking, farming, and easy travel. The rivers connected different groups and helped them share ideas and cultures. People have used these rivers like roads for a very long time. They traveled on boats and traded goods. Towns grew up next to the rivers because people needed the water.

HOW RIVERS AFFECTED CULTURE

The rivers created rich soil perfect for farming yams, rice, and vegetables. Fishing became important for communities near the water. The Niger Delta, where the river meets the ocean, became home to many groups who developed special cultures around fishing and farming. Rivers helped spread languages, religions, and traditions across Nigeria. The rivers made the soil good for growing food. People learned to fish and farm. Different groups of people lived near the rivers and shared their ways of life.
