CLIMATE AND LANDFORMS OF NIGERIA

VOCABULARY

River: A large stream of water that flows to the ocean.

Delta: Where a river spreads out as it meets the ocean.

Savanna: Grassland with scattered trees.

Rainforest: A thick forest that gets lots of rain.

NIGERIA'S IMPORTANT RIVERS

Nigeria has two very important rivers that have shaped the country's history and culture. The Niger River is the longest river in Nigeria at 2,547 miles long. The Benue River is the Niger's main tributary. These rivers meet in a Y-shape and flow together to the Atlantic Ocean. Nigeria even got its name from the Niger River! Nigeria has two big rivers - the Niger River and the Benue River. They meet and flow to the ocean. Nigeria got its name from the Niger River.

HOW RIVERS SHAPED NIGERIA'S HISTORY

The Niger and Benue rivers have been like highways for thousands of years. People used boats to travel and trade along these rivers. Ancient kingdoms grew up along the riverbanks because the rivers provided water for drinking, farming, and transportation. The rivers connected different groups of people and helped them share ideas, goods, and culture. People have used these rivers like roads for a very long time. They traveled on boats and traded goods. Towns grew up next to the rivers because people needed the water.

HOW RIVERS AFFECTED CULTURE

The rivers created rich, fertile soil perfect for farming. This allowed people to grow crops like yams, rice, and vegetables. Fishing became an important way of life for many communities. The Niger Delta, where the river meets the ocean, became home to many different ethnic groups who developed unique cultures based on fishing and farming. The rivers also helped spread languages, religions, and traditions across Nigeria. The rivers made the soil good for growing food. People learned to fish and farm. Different groups of people lived near the rivers and shared their ways of life.

NIGERIA'S DIFFERENT ENVIRONMENTS

Nigeria has different environmental regions from north to south. The coastal wetlands in the south have mangrove swamps where trees can live in salty water. Moving inland, there are tropical rainforests that get over 100 inches of rain each year. The middle of Nigeria has savannas with tall grass and scattered trees. The far north is drier with less rain, more like a desert. Nigeria has different environments. The south has swamps and thick forests. The middle has grasslands. The north is dry like a desert.

NIGERIA'S CLIMATE

Nigeria has a warm climate with two main seasons - wet and dry. The wet season brings heavy rains from April to October. The dry season lasts from November to March. The climate changes from very wet in the south to very dry in the north. This affects what plants can grow and how people live in different parts of the country. Nigeria is warm all year. It has a rainy season and a dry season. The south gets more rain than the north.
