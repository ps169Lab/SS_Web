CLIMATE AND LANDFORMS OF NIGERIA

VOCABULARY

River: A big stream of water that flows all the way to the ocean.

Delta: A place where a river splits into smaller streams as it reaches the ocean.

Savanna: A grassland with some trees spread out here and there.

Rainforest: A really thick forest that gets tons of rain and stays very green.

NIGERIA'S DIFFERENT ENVIRONMENTS

Nigeria is a country in West Africa. The land looks very different from the south to the north. In the south, near the ocean, there are swamps with mangrove trees that grow in salty water. Just above the coast, in the southeast and southwest, there are thick rainforests where it rains a lot. 

In the middle part of the country, there are wide grasslands called savannas. These areas have tall grass and a few trees. 

 The north is much drier and hotter, with sandy soil and little rain. The land there looks almost like a desert. As you move from the south to the north, the land gets drier and the trees become fewer. Nigeria is in West Africa. The land changes from the south to the north. The south has swamps and trees that grow in salty water. Above that, the southeast and southwest have rainforests with lots of rain. The middle has grasslands with tall grass and some trees. The north is hot and dry with little rain and few plants. The land gets drier as you go north.


NIGERIA'S CLIMATE

Nigeria stays warm year-round with two seasons. The wet season brings heavy rains from April to October. The dry season runs from November to March. Climate varies by location - the south gets lots of rain while the north stays dry. This affects what plants grow and how people live. Nigeria is warm all year. It has a rainy season and a dry season. The south gets more rain than the north.
