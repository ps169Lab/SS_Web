WILDLIFE OF NIGERIA

VOCABULARY

Adaptation: Special features that help animals and plants survive.

Biome: A large area with similar plants, animals, and weather.

Endangered: When there are very few of an animal left.

Habitat: The natural home where an animal or plant lives.

MANGROVE TREES

Mangrove trees live in the coastal wetlands of southern Nigeria, especially around the Niger River and Cross River deltas. These amazing trees have special adaptations that let them live in salty water where most other trees cannot survive. Their roots stick up out of the water to get air, and they can filter salt out of the seawater they drink. Mangrove trees live in the swamps of southern Nigeria. They can live in salty water. Their roots stick up to get air.

Mangrove trees are very important to Nigeria's ecosystem. They provide homes for fish, birds, and other animals. The Niger Delta mangrove swamps are the breeding ground for 40% of the fish caught in the ocean nearby. These trees also protect the coast from big waves and storms. Mangrove trees are important because they give homes to fish and birds. They also protect the land from big waves.

THE CROSS RIVER GORILLA

The Cross River Gorilla is one of Nigeria's most special animals, but it is critically endangered. Only about 300 of these gorillas are left in the world! They live in the dense rainforests along the border between Nigeria and Cameroon. These gorillas are adapted to life in the thick forest where they swing through trees and eat leaves, fruits, and bark. The Cross River Gorilla is very rare. Only 300 are left! They live in the thick forests between Nigeria and Cameroon. They eat leaves and fruits.

Cross River Gorillas have strong arms and hands that help them climb trees and move through the forest. They live in small family groups and are very shy around people. It has a golden crown of feathers and long legs. It lives in grasslands and wetlands.

NIGERIA'S DIFFERENT ENVIRONMENTS

Nigeria has many different biomes that provide homes for different animals and plants. The coastal wetlands in the south have mangrove swamps. Moving inland, there are tropical rainforests with thick trees. The middle of Nigeria has savannas with grasslands and scattered trees. Each environment has animals and plants with special adaptations to survive there. Nigeria has different environments. The south has swamps. The middle has thick forests. The north has grasslands. Each place has different animals and plants.
