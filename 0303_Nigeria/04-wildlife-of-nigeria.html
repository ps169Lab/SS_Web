WILDLIFE OF NIGERIA

INTRODUCTION

Nigeria has amazing wildlife and many different environments. Animals live in swamps, forests, and grasslands.

VOCABULARY

Adaptation: Special features that help animals and plants survive.

Biome: A large area with similar plants, animals, and weather.

Endangered: When there are very few of an animal left.

Habitat: The natural home where an animal or plant lives.

MANGROVE TREES

Mangrove trees live in the swamps of southern Nigeria. They can live in salty water. Their roots stick up to get air.

Mangrove trees are important because they give homes to fish and birds. They also protect the land from big waves.


THE CROSS RIVER GORILLA

The Cross River Gorilla is very rare. Only 300 are left! They live in the thick forests between Nigeria and Cameroon. They eat leaves and fruits.

These gorillas have strong arms for climbing. They live in small families and are shy. Their dark fur helps them hide in the forest.


THE BLACK-CROWNED CRANE

The Black-crowned Crane is Nigeria's national bird. It has a golden crown of feathers and long legs. It lives in grasslands and wetlands.

These cranes eat fish, frogs, and seeds. Their long necks help them find food. They can fly far to find food and water.


NIGERIA'S DIFFERENT ENVIRONMENTS

Nigeria has different environments. The south has swamps. The middle has thick forests. The north has grasslands.

PROTECTING NIGERIA'S WILDLIFE

Many animals are in danger because their homes are being destroyed. People are working to protect them by making parks and teaching others to care about wildlife.

CONCLUSION

Nigeria has amazing animals like gorillas and cranes. We also have elephants, hippos, and pangolins. We must protect them and their homes.
