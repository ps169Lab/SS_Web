The False Face Society & Wampum

Exploring the Sacred Tools of the Iroquois

Expert Vocabulary

  • False Face: A sacred wooden mask used by the Iroquois for healing ceremonies.
  • Hiawatha Belt: A famous wampum belt that records the peace between the Five Nations.
  • Covenant: A serious, sacred promise or agreement.

The False Face Society: Healing the Spirit

The False Face Society was a secret group of healers. When an Iroquois person felt sick or had a bad dream, they called upon this society for help. The most unique part of this tradition was how the masks were made: they were carved directly into the trunk of a living basswood tree! The False Face Society was a group of healers. They wore wooden masks. These masks were carved into the side of a living tree!

Before carving, the artist would pray and offer tobacco to the tree's spirit. They believed the spirit of the tree stayed inside the mask to help fight away "evil spirits" that caused sickness. Once finished, the masks were painted red if they were started in the morning, or black if they were started in the afternoon. The artist prayed to the tree before carving. They believed the mask had a spirit. Red masks were started in the morning. Black masks were started in the afternoon.

Wampum: The Living Record

To the Haudenosaunee (Iroquois), wampum was not moneyâ€”it was a way to "write" without a pen. These beads were made from the purple and white shells of the quahog clam found in the East. By weaving these beads into specific patterns on belts, the tribes could record history, laws, and sacred promises called covenants. Wampum are beads made from sea shells. They are purple and white. The beads were woven into belts to tell stories and keep laws.

The most famous record is the Hiawatha Belt. It shows four squares and a central white pine tree connected by a line. This pattern records the Great Law of Peace, which joined five warring tribes into one peaceful group. When a leader spoke while holding a belt, it was a sign that they were telling the absolute truth. The Hiawatha Belt is very famous. It shows five groups of people living in peace. Holding a wampum belt meant you were telling the truth.
