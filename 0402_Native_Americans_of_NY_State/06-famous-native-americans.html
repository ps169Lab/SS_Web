Famous Native Americans

Important leaders and figures who shaped Native American history

Vocabulary

  • Peacemaker: Someone who brings peace between groups
  • Prophet: A person who receives messages from spirits
  • Oratory: The skill of public speaking
  • Legend: A story passed down through generations

The Founders of the Iroquois Confederacy

Long before Europeans came to America, several important Native American leaders worked together to create peace among the warring tribes of New York. These leaders founded the Iroquois Confederacy, one of the first democratic governments in North America.


The Great Peacemaker (Deganawida)

The Great Peacemaker was a prophet who had a vision of peace among all the tribes. His Mohawk name, Skén:nen rahá:wi, means "Two River Currents Flowing Together." According to legend, he traveled from village to village, spreading his message that tribes should stop fighting and work together.


One famous story tells how the Great Peacemaker proved his spiritual power to the Mohawk people. He climbed a tree high above Cohoes Falls and told warriors to chop it down. Everyone watched him disappear into the rushing water and thought he had died. But the next morning, they found him sitting by a campfire, completely unharmed. Hiawatha

Hiawatha was an Onondaga leader known for his powerful speaking skills. He became the Great Peacemaker's most important helper in bringing the tribes together. Hiawatha is credited with inventing wampum belts, which were used to comfort chiefs and clan mothers who had lost family members in wars.


Working together, the Great Peacemaker and Hiawatha convinced five tribes - the Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca - to join the Iroquois Confederacy and live in peace. Jigonhsasee (The Mother of Nations)

Jigonhsasee was the Great Peacemaker's first ally and is considered a co-founder of the Iroquois Confederacy. Her home served as a neutral meeting place where leaders from rival tribes could come together safely to discuss peace.


Her important role shows how Native American women had significant power and influence in their communities. She is especially remembered in the oral traditions passed down by Native American women. The Legacy of These Leaders

These famous Native Americans created a system of government based on peace, cooperation, and respect for all people. The Great Law of Peace that guided the Iroquois Confederacy influenced the founding fathers when they created the United States Constitution.


The Iroquois Confederacy lasted for hundreds of years and showed that different groups could work together while keeping their own identities and traditions.
