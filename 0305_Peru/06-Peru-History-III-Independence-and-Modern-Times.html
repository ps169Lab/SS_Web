A New Nation

The Story of Peru's Freedom and Early Growth

Expert Vocabulary

  • Independence: Freedom from being ruled by another country.
  • Liberator: A leader who helps set people free.
  • Provinces: The areas of land outside of the big, busy cities (the countryside).
  • Guano: Seabird droppings that are used as a powerful fertilizer to help plants grow.

The Fight for Freedom

For a long time, the country of Peru was ruled by Spain. But in the early 1800s, the people of Peru decided they wanted to be free! Two famous liberators, named José de San Martín and Simón Bolívar, led brave armies to help Peru win its independence. It took many years of hard work and big battles, but in 1821, Peru proudly declared that it was a brand-new, free country. Long ago, Spain ruled Peru. Then, the people of Peru wanted to be free. Two brave leaders named José de San Martín and Simón Bolívar brought armies to help. In 1821, Peru won the fight and became its own free country!

A Divided Land: Cities vs. Provinces

The fight for freedom showed a big divide between the cities and the countryside. In big cities like Lima, many wealthy leaders actually wanted to stay under Spain's rule to protect their money. But out in the mountain provinces, country families fought hard for freedom. After winning, it was difficult to get everyone to agree on the new rules because life in the high mountains was so different from life in coastal cities. Because they disagreed, the government was very rocky and changed leaders often. The people did not always agree. Leaders in big cities like Lima wanted to keep things the same. But families living in the mountain provinces wanted to change things and be free. Life in the mountains was very different from life in the city. Because it was hard to agree, the government had many arguments and changed leaders a lot.

Finding Wealth and Growing Strong

Even though the government was a bit messy at first, Peru began to find its footing and grow! The country discovered it had amazing treasures to sell to the rest of the world. Peru exported rich silver from its mines, warm wool from alpacas, and a special treasure called guano from the coast. Other countries paid a lot of money for these items. This extra money helped Peru build its very first railroads, string up telegraph wires, and make its cities modern and bright. Peru started to grow and get strong. The country sold valuable things to the world, like silver from mines and warm wool from alpacas. They also sold guano, which helped farmers in other countries grow food. Peru used this money to build its first trains and fix up its towns!
