Independence and Modern Peru

Becoming a Free Republic

Expert Vocabulary

Independence
When a country is free to make its own rules and is not ruled by another country.
Republic
A country where the people choose their leaders by voting.
Revolution
A big, often sudden change in how a country is run.
Guano
Bird droppings used as fertilizer to help plants grow.

After 300 years of Spanish rule, Peruvians wanted to be in charge of their own land. This led to a long fight for independence. Today, Peru is a republic, which means the people elect their own President and leaders. Peru was ruled by Spain for a long time. Then, Peru fought a war to be free. Now, Peru is its own country. The people get to vote for their leaders.

The Heroes of Freedom

Two famous generals helped Peru become free: José de San Martín and Simón Bolívar. San Martín officially declared Peru free in Lima on July 28, 1821. However, the fighting didn't fully end until 1824 at the Battle of Ayacucho. Two brave men helped Peru. Their names were San Martín and Bolívar. They led armies to win against the Spanish. July 28 is now Peru’s big Independence Day!

The Flag

Red and white. Red stands for the blood of heroes, and white stands for peace.

National Anthem

A special song called "Somos Libres" (We Are Free).

The "Bird Poop" Boom!

In the mid-1800s, Peru became very wealthy by selling Guano (bird poop) to other countries. Because it was such a great fertilizer for farms, countries all over the world paid a lot of money for it! Peru used this money to build its first railroads. Peru once got very rich by selling bird poop! It is called Guano. Farmers in other countries used it to help their plants grow. Peru used the money to build trains and schools.

Modern Challenges and Growth

The 1900s: Peru built many new roads and factories, but there were many arguments about who should be the leader. Sometimes the military took control.

1980 to 2000: Peru faced hard times with an unfair economy and scary groups that caused trouble. A president named Fujimori fixed the money problems but also broke many rules.

Today: Peru is much more peaceful. While there are still arguments in the government, more children are going to school and the country is famous for its amazing food and tourism!

Peru Today

Even though Peru has faced many challenges, it is now one of the most famous travel spots in the world. People come from every country to see the ancient Inca ruins, eat delicious Peruvian food, and see the beautiful Amazon rainforest. Peruvians are proud of their history and their freedom. Peru is a wonderful place today. Many people visit to see the old Inca cities and the jungle. Peruvians love their country and are proud to be free.
