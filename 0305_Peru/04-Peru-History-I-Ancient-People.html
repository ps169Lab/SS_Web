Ancient People of Peru

From First Settlers to the Great Inca Empire

Expert Vocabulary

Civilization
An organized group of people with their own rules, art, and ways of building.
Empire
A very large area of land and many different groups of people ruled by one leader.
Inca
The largest and most powerful empire in ancient South America.
Quipu
A tool made of knotted strings used by the Inca to keep records and count things.

Surviving in a Tough Environment

People have lived in the land we call Peru for over 15,000 years! Long before there were modern cities, ancient groups learned how to survive in Peru's extreme environments. By learning how to master the dry coastal deserts and the freezing mountain peaks, these early civilizations laid the foundation for the famous Inca Empire. People have lived in Peru for a very long time. They learned how to hunt, farm, and build in the dry deserts and high mountains. Many different groups lived there before the famous Inca started.

Life in the Coastal Desert

The first people in Peru settled near the ocean. In ancient villages like Huaca Prieta, people used local cotton to weave fishing nets to catch food from the Pacific Ocean. Even though the coast was a very dry desert, these early settlers grew crops by using rivers that flowed down from the nearby Andes mountains to water their fields. Over time, groups like the Norte Chico culture built massive stone pyramids right in the sand. The first people lived by the ocean. There was very little rain in the desert, but they got water from rivers that ran down from the mountains. They used cotton to make fishing nets and built large stone pyramids in the sand.

Advanced Desert and Mountain Societies

As populations grew, people spread to new areas and created incredible art. In the dry southern deserts, the Nazca people scraped away dark stones to draw giant lines and animal pictures in the sand that can only be fully seen from the sky! Meanwhile, high up in the mountain region, the Chavin culture adapted to colder weather and steeper land, carving massive stone temples and beautiful artwork of jungle predators like jaguars. Later, people spread across the land. In the flat desert, the Nazca people drew giant pictures of animals in the sand. High in the cold mountains, the Chavin group built huge stone temples with carvings of jaguars.

The Mighty Inca Empire

The Inca became the most famous and powerful group by combining the clever skills of all the older cultures. Led by powerful rulers like the Emperor Pachacuti, their massive empire stretched all the way through the rugged Andes Mountains. They called their land "Tawantinsuyu," which means "The Land of the Four Parts," because it connected different geographical regions and unique groups of people under one capital city: Cusco. The Inca were the biggest and strongest group. Rulers like Pachacuti used good ideas from older groups to build a massive empire across the mountains. Their capital city was Cusco.

Inca Achievements: Solving Mountain Problems

Governing a massive mountain empire made farming, communication, and construction incredibly difficult. The Inca invented brilliant engineering strategies to conquer these environmental challenges: It is hard to live on steep mountains. The Inca invented clever ways to solve their environmental problems:

  • Terrace Farming: They carved giant green "steps" into steep mountainsides to create flat land for growing potatoes and corn.
  • The Inca Trail: They built a huge network of stone roads and rope bridges to connect the desert coast to the high mountain cities.
  • Quipus: Since they did not write on paper, they used special knotted strings to count items and keep records across the empire.
  • Stone Building: They built famous mountain cities like Machu Picchu using heavy stones that fit together so perfectly that they did not need any cement.

Class Discussion Questions

  • The Environment Challenge: Think about Peru's geography. Why would building a road over a flat desert coast be easier than building a road through the high mountain region? How did the Inca solve this?
  • Record Keeping: How would using strings and knots (Quipus) to remember numbers be helpful for a messenger running along the Inca Trail?
  • Learning from the Past: Look at the terrace "steps" the Inca carved into the mountains. How did changing the shape of the land help them grow enough food for a whole empire?
