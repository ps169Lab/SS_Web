Ancient People of Peru

From First Settlers to the Great Inca Empire

Expert Vocabulary

Civilization
An organized group of people with their own rules, art, and ways of building.
Empire
A very large area of land and many different groups of people ruled by one leader.
Inca
The largest and most powerful empire in ancient South America.
Quipu
A tool made of knotted strings used by the Inca to keep records and count things.

People have lived in the land we call Peru for over 15,000 years! Long before there were modern cities, ancient groups learned how to survive in the desert and the mountains. These early civilizations laid the foundation for the famous Inca Empire. People have lived in Peru for a very long time. They learned how to hunt, farm, and build in the mountains. Many different groups lived there before the famous Inca Empire started.

Timeline of Ancient Peru

Group When They Lived What They Built or Made
Norte Chico 5,000 years ago The oldest pyramids in the Americas.
Chavin 3,000 years ago Stone temples and art showing jaguars and snakes.
The Inca 600 years ago A massive empire with long roads and Machu Picchu.

The Earliest Settlers

The first people in Peru were hunters and gatherers. Around 7,000 years ago, they began to stay in one place to farm. They used cotton to make fishing nets and started building small villages along the coast. One of the oldest sites is called Huaca Prieta. The first people hunted for food. Later, they learned to grow plants. They stayed in one place and built small homes. They used cotton to make nets for fishing in the ocean.

The First Advanced Societies

As the population grew, people built bigger things. The Norte Chico people built massive temples and pyramids. Later, the Chavin people made beautiful pottery and art. They even created the giant Nazca Lines in the desert! Small groups turned into big cities. People built giant stone temples. They made beautiful art and pottery. Some groups even drew the giant pictures in the desert sand.

The Mighty Inca Empire

The Inca were the most famous and powerful group. At its peak, their empire stretched through the Andes Mountains from Ecuador to Chile. They called their land "Tawantinsuyu," which means "The Land of the Four Parts." The Inca were the biggest and strongest group. Their land was huge! It went across many mountains. They had a big capital city called Cusco.

Inca Achievements

  • The Inca Trail: A huge network of roads that connected the whole empire.
  • Terrace Farming: Building giant "steps" on steep mountains to grow food.
  • Quipus: Using knotted strings to count and keep records because they didn't have a written language.
  • Stone Building: Building cities like Machu Picchu with stones that fit perfectly without any cement.

The End of the Empire

The Inca Empire faced big challenges when two brothers, Huascar and Atahualpa, fought over who should be leader. While the empire was weak, soldiers from Spain arrived. The Spanish had fewer men, but they had different weapons and armor. By 1572, the Spanish had taken control of the Inca lands. Two brothers in the Inca family fought each other. This made the empire weak. At the same time, soldiers from Spain came across the ocean. The Spanish eventually took over the land.

Class Discussion Questions

  • Record Keeping: How would you feel about using strings and knots (Quipus) to remember important information instead of writing it down?
  • Architecture: Why is it impressive that the Inca built Machu Picchu without using any glue or cement to hold the stones together?
  • Learning from the Past: Look at the "steps" (terraces) the Inca built. How did that help them solve the problem of living on a steep mountain?
