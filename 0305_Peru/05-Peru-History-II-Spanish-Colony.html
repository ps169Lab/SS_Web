Peru as a Spanish Colony

The Age of the Viceroyalty (1532–1824)

Expert Vocabulary

Viceroyalty
A colony ruled by a "Viceroy," who was a leader chosen to act in place of the King.
Conquistador
A Spanish word for "conqueror"—the soldiers who took over the land.
Colonial
The time period when one country (like Spain) rules over another land (like Peru).
Rebellion
When people fight back against the leaders in charge to try and change the rules.

The Colonial Period began when Spanish soldiers arrived in Peru in 1532. For nearly 300 years, Peru was no longer ruled by the Inca Emperors. Instead, it became a Viceroyalty of Spain. This changed everything from the language people spoke to the way the cities were built. About 500 years ago, soldiers from Spain came to Peru. This was the start of the Colonial time. Peru was now ruled by the King of Spain instead of the Inca. This lasted for a long time!

The Spanish Conquest

A Spanish leader named Francisco Pizarro led a small group of 168 men into Peru. Even though the Inca army was much larger, the Spanish had horses and metal armor. Because the Inca Empire was already weak from a civil war between two brothers, the Spanish were able to capture the Emperor and take control of the land. A man named Pizarro led the Spanish soldiers. They had horses and strong armor. The Inca were already fighting among themselves, so it was easier for the Spanish to take over.

The City of Kings

The Spanish moved the capital from the mountains (Cusco) to the coast. They built a new city called Lima. It became a very fancy and powerful city. The Spanish King sent a Viceroy to live there and run the government, making sure the gold and silver were sent back to Spain. The Spanish built a new capital city called Lima. It was right by the ocean. The Viceroy lived there and made the rules for the King.

Economy: Mining for Treasure

Spain wanted Peru's natural resources to become wealthy. They forced local people to work in deep mines to dig for silver and gold. This was a very hard job, but it made Lima one of the richest cities in the world during the 1700s. The Spanish wanted gold and silver. They made the local people dig in the mountains for treasure. It was like playing Minecraft in real life, but it was very hard and dangerous work.

Mixing Cultures and Fighting Back

Over time, Spanish and Native cultures began to mix. This created new styles of art, music, and food. However, many people were unhappy with Spanish rule. Great leaders like Túpac Amaru II led rebellions to try and win back their freedom. Spanish and Native ways mixed together to make new music and art. But many people did not like the Spanish rules. They fought in big battles to try to be free again.

The Road to Independence

By the early 1800s, people all across South America were fighting for independence. Famous generals like José de San Martín helped lead the fight in Peru. After many years of war, Peru finally became a free country in 1824. After a long time, Peru fought a big war to be free. A man named San Martín helped them. In 1824, Peru finally became its own country!
