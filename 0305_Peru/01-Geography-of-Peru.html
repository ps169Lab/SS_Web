The Geography of Peru

A Land of Three Worlds

Expert Vocabulary

Andes
A large mountain range that runs through Peru and divides the country into three regions.
Biome
A large area of land with its own special weather, plants, and animals.
Climate
The usual weather in a place over a long period of time.
Equator
An imaginary line around the middle of the Earth where it is usually very warm.

Where is Peru Located?

Peru is a large country located on the western side of South America, right next to the Pacific Ocean. It shares borders with five other countries: Ecuador, Colombia, Brazil, Bolivia, and Chile. Because Peru is near the equator, it has many different climates—from snowy mountain peaks to hot jungles! Peru is a big country in South America. It sits next to the Pacific Ocean. Peru is near the middle of the Earth, called the equator. Peru has many kinds of weather because of its high mountains.

The Three Regions of Peru

The most important thing to know about Peru’s geography is that the Andes Mountains split the country into three different sections. Imagine the country as three long strips of land, each with its own look and feel. The giant Andes mountains divide Peru into three special parts. Each part has its own plants, animals, and weather.


Comparing the Three Regions
Region Name What it Looks Like The Climate (Weather)
The Coast (Costa) A narrow, dry strip of desert next to the ocean. Mild and damp, but almost never rains.
The Highlands (Sierra) Giant, snowy peaks in the Andes Mountains. Cold and crisp with rainy summers.
The Jungle (Selva) Flat, thick rainforest that covers 60% of Peru. Very hot and very rainy all year.

The Coast (Costa)

The Coast is a narrow strip of land along the Pacific Ocean. Most of it is a dry desert, but it has valleys made by rivers flowing from the mountains. The ocean provides many fish and sea animals for the people of Peru. The coast is a dry, sandy area next to the ocean. It does not rain much here. The ocean gives Peru many fish to eat.

The Highlands (Sierra)

The Highlands are home to the snowy peaks of the Andes. As you go higher up the mountains, the air gets much colder. This region blocks the clouds from reaching the coast, which is why the coast stays so dry. The Highlands are high up in the mountains. It is very cold and snowy at the top. The big mountains block the rain from reaching the coast.

The Amazon Jungle (Selva)

The Jungle is a massive rainforest that covers more than half of the country. It is very hot and gets a lot of rain. The mighty Amazon River starts here and flows all the way to the Atlantic Ocean. The Jungle is a big, thick forest. It is very hot and rainy. A huge river called the Amazon starts in this part of Peru.

Natural Resources and Population

Peru is rich in natural resources like copper, gold, and silver. Most people (59%) live in cities along the Coast, while others live in the mountains or jungle. Over thousands of years, people have adapted to the land. Ancient groups like the Incas built "steps" or terraces into the mountains to grow food. Peru has many treasures like gold and silver in the ground. Most people live in cities by the ocean. Long ago, people learned to grow food on big "steps" cut into the hills.

Geography Fun Facts

  • The Amazon River starts in Peru!
  • Peru has 4% of all the fresh water on Earth.
  • Machu Picchu is a famous ancient city high on a mountain.
  • The first potatoes came from the Peruvian mountains.
