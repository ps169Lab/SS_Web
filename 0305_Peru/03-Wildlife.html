Wildlife of Peru

Nature’s Amazing Survivors

Expert Vocabulary

Adaptation
A special body part or behavior that helps a living thing survive in its home.
Altitude
The height of a place above the level of the ocean.
Megadiverse
A word used for countries that have a huge variety of different plants and animals.

Because Peru has three very different regions—the Coast, the Highlands, and the Jungle—the animals and plants here have had to develop incredible adaptations. To survive in the freezing mountains or the salty ocean, every living thing needs a special "survival kit." Peru has three main parts: the Coast, the Mountains, and the Jungle. In each part, animals and plants have special ways to stay alive. We call these adaptations.

Summary: How They Survive

Alpaca The Highlands (Mountains) Thick wool for warmth and special blood for breathing thin air.

Pelican Adaptations: The Coastal Fisherman

Pelicans along the Pacific Coast are built for fishing. They have huge throat pouches that stretch like rubber bands to scoop up three gallons of water and fish at once! Their feathers are waterproof, and their webbed feet make them excellent swimmers. Pelicans live by the ocean. They have a big pouch under their beak. It stretches out to catch lots of fish and water. Their feathers stay dry when they dive into the waves!

Alpaca Adaptations: The Mountain Traveler

High in the Andes, the air is thin and cold. Alpacas have thick, warm wool to survive freezing nights. They also have special blood that carries more oxygen, which helps them breathe at a high altitude where the air is "thinner." Alpacas live high in the mountains. They have very thick fur to stay warm. Their bodies are special because they can breathe easily in the high, thin mountain air.

Ichu Grass: The Tough Mountain Clump

Ichu grass is a survival expert. It grows in tight clumps to trap heat and stay warm. Its narrow, tough leaves won't break in strong mountain winds, and its deep roots search for water in the dry soil. This grass grows in the cold mountains. It grows in tight bunches to stay warm. Its roots go deep into the ground to find water.

More Amazing Wildlife

The Amazon Jungle (Selva)

In the hot, rainy jungle, Toucans have light, hollow beaks to reach fruit, and Jaguars use spots to camouflage (hide) in the leaves.

The Highland Wonders

The Andean Condor has giant wings to soar through mountain air, while the Spectacled Bear has thick fur to stay cozy.

The Coastal Life

Sea Lions have thick fat to swim in cold ocean water, and Carob trees have long roots to find water in the desert sand.

Peru is megadiverse, meaning it has one of the largest varieties of life on Earth. From over 1,800 types of birds to thousands of plants, every living thing has a story of how it adapted to its home. Peru has so many different plants and animals. It is one of the most diverse places on Earth! Every creature has a special way of living in its home.
