Vocabulary

Adaptation
Special body parts or behaviors that help animals and plants survive
Biome
A large area with similar climate, plants, and animals
Altitude
How high a place is above sea level

Peru's Three Climate Regions

Peru has three main climate regions: the Coast, the Highlands, and the Amazon Jungle. In each region, plants and animals have special adaptations to help them survive. Let's learn about three amazing examples: pelicans, alpacas, and ichu grass!


Pelican Adaptations in the Coastal Desert


Pelicans live along Peru's Pacific Coast. They have amazing adaptations to help them catch fish and survive by the ocean.

  • Pelicans have huge pouches under their beaks that can hold up to 3 gallons of water and fish!
  • Their special throat pouches stretch like rubber bands to scoop up fish.
  • Pelicans have waterproof feathers that keep them dry when diving into water.
  • They have webbed feet that help them swim and take off from water.
  • Pelicans have excellent eyesight to spot fish from high in the air.

Alpaca Adaptations in the Highlands

Alpacas live in the Andes Mountains of Peru. They have special adaptations to survive in cold, high places where there isn't much oxygen.

  • Alpacas have thick, warm wool that keeps them cozy in freezing temperatures.
  • Their blood can carry more oxygen than other animals, helping them live high in the mountains.
  • Alpacas have special padded feet that don't damage the ground when they walk.
  • They have long necks to reach grass without bending down in the snow.
  • Alpacas can go a long time without water, which helps in dry mountain areas.

Ichu Grass Adaptations in the Highlands

Ichu grass grows in the high Andes Mountains. It has special adaptations to survive harsh mountain weather.

  • Ichu grass has tough, narrow leaves that don't break in strong winds.
  • It grows in tight clumps to trap heat and stay warm.
  • The grass has deep roots to find water in dry mountain soil.
  • Ichu grass can survive freezing temperatures that would kill other plants.
  • Its silvery color reflects sunlight to protect it from strong mountain sun.

People and Wildlife in Peru

Just like plants and animals, people in Peru have adapted to live in different regions. In the mountains, people use alpaca wool to make warm clothes. They also use ichu grass to build roofs for their homes. Along the coast, people catch fish like pelicans do, but with boats and nets instead of beaks!


The special places where plants and animals live help decide where people build their homes too. People learn from wildlife and find ways to live in all of Peru's different regions.


More Amazing Wildlife in Peru

Peru has so many other amazing animals and plants! Here are a few more examples from each region:

More Coast Animals and Plants

  • Sea lions have thick fat to stay warm in the cold Humboldt Current
  • Anchovies swim in huge groups to stay safe from bigger fish
  • Carob trees have very long roots to find water in the desert
  • Mangrove trees can grow in salty water that would kill other plants

More Highlands Animals and Plants

  • The Andean condor has huge wings that help it fly in thin mountain air
  • Spectacled bears have thick fur to stay warm in cold mountains
  • Guinea pigs have round bodies that keep heat in
  • The Puya raimondii plant can live even when it's freezing

More Amazon Jungle Animals and Plants

  • Jaguars have spotted fur that helps them hide in the forest
  • Toucans have big, light beaks to reach fruit on thin branches
  • Poison dart frogs are bright colors to warn other animals they are dangerous
  • Orchids grow on trees to get sunlight in the dense jungle

Peru is one of the most "megadiverse" countries in the world. This means it has lots and lots of different plants and animals. Peru has over 1,800 kinds of birds and 500 kinds of mammals. It also has over 21,000 different plants and animals in total!
