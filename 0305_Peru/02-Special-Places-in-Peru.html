Special Places in Peru

Exploring the Wonders of the Land

Expert Vocabulary

Vinicunca
Also known as Rainbow Mountain; a mountain with colorful stripes made of minerals.
Geoglyphs
Giant designs or pictures made on the ground by moving rocks and soil.
Oasis
A small, green area in the middle of a desert where there is water.
Preserve
To keep something safe so it stays the same for a long time.

Peru is filled with breathtaking landmarks that tell the story of its land and people. From mountains that look like rainbows to giant mystery drawings in the desert sand, there is something amazing to see in every region. Peru has many special places to see. Some are colorful mountains. Others are giant pictures in the sand. Every part of Peru has a special story to tell.

Vinicunca: The Rainbow Mountain

Deep in the Andes Mountains sits Vinicunca. For thousands of years, this mountain was hidden under thick ice. When the ice melted, it revealed beautiful stripes of red, gold, and turquoise. These colors come from different minerals in the earth. Rainbow Mountain is high in the mountains. It has colorful stripes in the dirt. The colors come from minerals in the ground. It was hidden under ice for a long time!

The Mysterious Nazca Lines

In the dry desert of the coast, you will find geoglyphs called the Nazca Lines. These are massive drawings of animals like monkeys, whales, and spiders. Because it almost never rains or blows wind in this desert, the lines have been preserved for over 1,500 years! The Nazca Lines are giant pictures in the desert sand. People made them a long time ago. You can see a spider, a monkey, and even a whale! The dry weather keeps the pictures safe.

Machu Picchu: The Lost City

Machu Picchu is a famous city built by the Incas in the 1400s. It is known for its incredible stonework. The Incas fit giant stones together so perfectly that you can't even fit a piece of paper between them—and they didn't use any cement! Machu Picchu is an old city on top of a mountain. It was built by the Incas. They used big stones to build the walls. The stones fit together perfectly like a puzzle.

Important Cities

Lima (The Coast)

Lima is the capital city. It sits by the ocean and is a mix of very old Spanish buildings and modern skyscrapers.

Cusco (The Highlands)

Cusco was the capital of the Inca Empire. Today, you can still see Inca stone walls at the bottom of the Spanish buildings.

Iquitos (The Jungle)

Iquitos is in the heart of the rainforest. It is the largest city in the world that you cannot reach by car. You must take a plane or a boat!

More Cool Spots!

  • Huacachina: A tiny village built around a blue oasis in the middle of giant sand dunes.
  • Colca Canyon: A massive canyon that is twice as deep as the Grand Canyon in the USA!
  • Chan Chan: An ancient city made entirely out of mud bricks.

Class Discussion Questions

  • The Mystery: Why do you think the Nazca people made drawings that were too big to see from the ground?
  • No Roads: What would it be like to live in a city like Iquitos where there are no roads? How would you get your groceries?
  • Ancient Builders: How do you think the Incas moved those giant stones to the top of a mountain without trucks or machines?
