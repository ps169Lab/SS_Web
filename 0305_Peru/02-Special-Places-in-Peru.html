Special Places in Peru

Exploring the Wonders of the Land

Expert Vocabulary

Geoglyphs
Giant designs or pictures made on the ground by moving rocks and soil.
Oasis
A small, green area in the middle of a desert where there is water.
Altitude
The height of a place above the level of the sea (like high up in the mountains).

Peru is filled with breathtaking landmarks that tell the story of its land and people. Each of these special places belongs to one of the three climate regions we have studied: the Coastal Desert, the Highlands, and the Rainforest. Peru has many special places. Each place is in one of the three regions: the Desert, the Mountains, or the Jungle. Let’s explore them!

Region 1: The Coastal Desert

The Mysterious Nazca Lines

In the dry desert of the coast, you will find geoglyphs called the Nazca Lines. Because it almost never rains in the coastal region, these giant pictures of monkeys and spiders have been preserved for over 1,500 years! The Nazca Lines are giant pictures in the desert sand. Because it is so dry in the coastal desert, the pictures stay safe for a long time.

Also in this region: Lima (The Capital) and Huacachina (A desert oasis).

Region 2: The Highlands (The Andes)

Machu Picchu: The Lost City

Machu Picchu is an ancient city built high in the Andes Mountains. The Incas used the stone from the mountains to build incredible walls. In the Highlands, the air is thin and the mountains are very steep. Machu Picchu is an old city on top of a mountain. It was built by the Incas using big stones. It is very high up in the mountains!

Vinicunca: The Rainbow Mountain

Also in the Highlands sits Vinicunca. For thousands of years, this mountain was hidden under thick ice. When the ice melted, it revealed stripes of red, gold, and turquoise minerals. Rainbow Mountain is high in the mountains. It has colorful stripes in the dirt. It was hidden under ice for a long time!

Also in this region: Cusco (The ancient Inca capital).

Region 3: The Rainforest (The Amazon)

Iquitos: The Jungle City

Iquitos is located deep in the heart of the Amazon Jungle. It is the largest city in the world that has no roads leading to it! Because the rainforest is so thick and filled with rivers, you must take a plane or a boat to get there. Iquitos is a city in the jungle. There are no roads to get there! You have to take a boat or a plane because the jungle is so thick.

Also in this region: The Amazon River and thousands of types of animals.

  • Traveling: If you wanted to visit Iquitos and Machu Picchu, what two different types of weather would you need to pack for?
  • The Highlands: Why do you think the Incas built their cities high in the mountains instead of in the flat coastal desert?
