GRADE 4 SOCIAL STUDIES RESOURCES

Discover New York State history, government, and the founding of America

Welcome to Grade 4 Social Studies! These units are designed to help fourth-grade students learn about New York State geography, history, and government, as well as the founding of America. Each unit includes interesting facts, important vocabulary words, and images to help students understand these important topics.

New York State Geography

Learn about the geography of New York State, including its physical features, landforms, wildlife, political characteristics, and special places like New York City.

Explore NY Geography

Explorers and Colonists in New Netherlands

Discover the Age of Exploration, life on ships, major explorers, trading posts, the Dutch West India Company, and everyday life in New Amsterdam.

Explore New Netherlands

New Nation

Learn how the United States government was formed, including the Constitution, Bill of Rights, and how New York State played a role in the early nation.

Explore New Nation

New York Rights Movement

Discover the brave people who fought for freedom and equal rights in New York State, including slavery, the Underground Railroad, and women's suffrage.

Explore Rights Movement

FOR TEACHERS

These Grade 4 resources focus on New York State history and the founding of America, helping students develop an understanding of their state's role in American history and government. Each unit is designed to align with elementary social studies curriculum standards and includes grade-appropriate content with vocabulary definitions and visual aids to support student learning.
