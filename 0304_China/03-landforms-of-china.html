Landforms of China

Exploring the Mountains, Rivers, and Deserts

The High Mountains and Plateaus

China has some of the highest mountains in the world! The Himalayas have Mount Everest, the tallest peak on Earth. Nearby is the Tibetan Plateau, which people call "the roof of the world" because it is so high up. These areas are in Outer China and are very cold. China has very high mountains! The Himalayas have Mount Everest. The Tibetan Plateau is called "the roof of the world." These places are high and cold.

Many of China's rivers start in these mountains when the snow melts. Because the air is thin and the ground is rocky, people here raise yaks instead of growing crops. Rivers start here when snow melts. People raise yaks here because it is too cold to farm.

The Two Great Rivers

China has two famous rivers that act like life-lines for the country. Both start in the mountains, but they are very different! China has two famous rivers. They both start in the mountains.

The Yellow River (Huang He)

The Yellow River is famous for its yellow silt. This river is known for flooding often. While the floods can be dangerous, they leave behind fertile mud that is perfect for growing wheat. The Yellow River has yellow silt. This river floods a lot. The floods leave good mud for growing wheat.

The Yangtze River (Chang Jiang)

The Yangtze is the longest river in China. Unlike the Yellow River, it does not flood as dangerously. It flows through warm, wet basins, making it the perfect place to grow rice. The Yangtze is the longest river. It does not flood as much. It is warm and wet, so people grow rice here.

Deserts: The "Land of No Return"

The Gobi and Taklamakan deserts are large, dry places in Outer China. The Taklamakan is so dangerous people call it the "land of no return" because of its shifting sand dunes and lack of water. The deserts are dry and hot. One is called the "land of no return." It has lots of sand and no water.

In these dry areas, people live near oases. An oasis is a place in the desert where there is water. People use camels to travel across the sand because camels can go a long time without drinking. People in the desert live near water called an oasis. They use camels to travel.

How Landforms Shape Life

Landforms decide where people live. Most people live in the River Lands (Inner China) because of the fresh water and flat land for farming. Very few people live in the Extreme Lands (Outer China) because the mountains and deserts are too difficult to survive in. Landforms show people where to live. Most people live near rivers. Very few people live in the mountains or deserts.
