Ancient China Part 1: The Rivers and the First Rulers

How a Great Civilization Began

Expert Vocabulary

  • Dynasty: A family of rulers who lead a country for a long time.
  • Ancestor: A family member who lived a long time ago.
  • Mandate: An official order or a right to do something.

Life Near the Rivers

Thousands of years ago, the first people in China settled near two massive rivers: the Yellow River and the Yangtze River. These rivers provided fresh water for drinking and rich soil for farming. These early farmers grew crops like rice and millet. Because the rivers provided everything they needed to survive, people began to build permanent homes and villages, which eventually grew into the great civilization of China. Long ago, people in China lived near two big rivers. They were the Yellow River and the Yangtze River. The rivers gave them water and helped them grow food like rice. Because they had water and food, they stayed in one place and built towns.

The Shang and the Oracle Bones

The first major group we know about was the Shang Dynasty. They were famous for using bronze to make tools and for creating the first Chinese writing. The Shang used "Oracle Bones" to try to predict the future. They would write questions on animal bones or turtle shells, heat them until they cracked, and then "read" the cracks to find answers. This is the earliest example of writing in China's history! The Shang Dynasty was a group of early rulers. they were the first to write things down. They wrote on animal bones called "Oracle Bones." They used these bones to try to guess what would happen in the future.

The Zhou and the Mandate of Heaven

Later, the Zhou Dynasty took over. To explain why they should be in charge, they introduced the Mandate of Heaven. They believed that the heavens chose wise and kind rulers. If an emperor became mean or lazy, the heavens would take away their right to rule and give it to a better family. This idea helped people understand why different dynasties rose and fell over many years. The Zhou Dynasty became the new rulers. They said the heavens chose them because they were good. This was called the Mandate of Heaven. They believed that only good and kind leaders should be allowed to rule the people.

Wise Words from Confucius

During the Zhou Dynasty, a wise man named Confucius lived. He didn't want to invent new rules, but he wanted people to follow the good ways of the past. He taught that everyone should be respectful to their families and elders. His most famous teaching is similar to the "Golden Rule": never do to others what you would not want them to do to you. His ideas about kindness and honesty are still a major part of Chinese culture today. Confucius was a very wise teacher. He taught people to be kind and honest. He said children should respect their parents and older people. He wanted everyone to follow rules so that life would be peaceful and happy for everyone.

Class Discussion Questions

  • The River Mystery: Why do you think almost every early civilization started near a river? What could happen if the river dried up or flooded?
  • The Golden Rule: Confucius said we should treat others the way we want to be treated. How does that rule help a classroom stay peaceful?
  • Writing History: If we didn't have the Oracle Bones, how would we know what the Shang people were thinking about 3,000 years ago?
