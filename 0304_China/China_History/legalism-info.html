Legalism

Ancient China's System of Strict Rules & Strong Government

📜 Legalism was a set of ideas about how rulers should govern — using LAWS, POWER, and CONTROL to keep order.
💡 The Big Ideas of Legalism
📜
Rule by Law
Legalists believed in very strict laws. Clear rules and punishments were needed to keep everyone safe and orderly.
👑
Strong Power (Shi)
The ruler's power had to be total and complete. Nobody was allowed to challenge or question the ruler's authority.
🧠
Smart Control (Shu)
Rulers used clever methods to stay in control — including smart ways to manage officials and everyday people.
⚖️
Rewards & Punishments
Good behavior earned rewards. Breaking the rules brought harsh punishments. This kept people following the law.
🐉
🏛️ Legalism in Action: The Qin Dynasty
🤴
Qin Shi Huang — China's First Emperor

The Qin (say: "Chin") Dynasty was the best example of Legalism. Emperor Qin Shi Huang used Legalist ideas to rule a huge empire — making everything uniform, building great things, and keeping very strict control.

⚖️ Same Rules Everywhere

He made weights, money, and writing the same all across China. This made it much easier to trade and talk to people in faraway places.

🧱 Huge Building Projects

He built parts of the Great Wall of China, plus roads and canals. These huge projects showed just how powerful the government was.

⚔️ Very Strict Punishments

Laws were extremely tough. Even small mistakes could lead to big punishments. This made people afraid to break the rules.

🔥 Burning of Books

To control what people thought, he burned many books. Only books about farming, medicine, and fortune-telling were kept.

💥
Why Did the Qin Dynasty Fall Apart?

The Qin Dynasty was very powerful — but it only lasted about 15 years! The rules were so harsh that people became very angry. Citizens rebelled against the cruel government, and the whole dynasty fell apart. This shows that being too strict can backfire!

⏳ Only 15 years!
📚 Key Words to Know
Legalism= a set of rules for governing Dynasty= a family of rulers Emperor= the top ruler Punishment= consequence for breaking a rule Rebellion= fighting back against a ruler Uniform= the same everywhere
