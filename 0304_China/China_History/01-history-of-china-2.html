Ancient China Part 2: The First Emperor

From Many Kingdoms to One Great Country

Expert Vocabulary

  • Unite: To bring different parts together to make one whole.
  • Legalism: The belief that a powerful government and strict laws are the best way to keep order.
  • Standardize: To make things the same so they match across the whole country.

The Warring States Period

After the Zhou Dynasty, China entered a difficult time called the Warring States period. Instead of one ruler, many different kingdoms fought for hundreds of years to see who was the strongest. Most of this fighting happened in Inner China, near the fertile lands of the Yellow River. People wanted peace, but the kingdoms couldn't stop fighting until one group became strong enough to win them all. For a long time, China was not one country. It was made of many small kingdoms. These kingdoms fought each other in big wars. This was a scary time for people. Everyone wanted the fighting to stop so they could live in peace.

The Rise of the Qin

One kingdom, called the Qin (pronounced "Chin"), used a new idea called Legalism to grow powerful. They believed that clear, strict rules helped a community get things done. In 221 BC, their leader, King Zheng, defeated all the other kingdoms. He united China for the very first time and gave himself a new title: The First Emperor (Qin Shi Huang). Because he united the country, the name "China" actually comes from his dynasty, the Qin! The Qin kingdom became the strongest. Their leader was King Zheng. He won the wars and brought all the kingdoms together. He became the very First Emperor. He used strict rules to make sure everyone in the country did things the same way.

Standardizing the Country

Being the First Emperor was a massive job. To help the different parts of China work together, Qin Shi Huang standardized many things - this means he wanted everything to be the same for everyone. He made everyone use the same alphabet to write, and he made sure everyone used the same weights, measures, and money. This idea was called Legalism. Legalism made it much easier for a merchant in the south to trade with a farmer near the Yellow River because their money and scales finally matched! The Emperor wanted to make life easier for trade. He made the writing the same in every town. He also made sure that money and measuring tools were the same everywhere. This idea was called Legalism. Legalsim helped people buy and sell things to each other without getting confused.

The Great Wall

To the north of China lies the dry and rocky Gobi Desert. Invaders from the north often tried to attack the farms in Inner China. To stop them, the Emperor began building the Great Wall of China. He connected older walls together to create one giant barrier. It was built with stone and earth to protect the people and show the world how powerful China had become. The Emperor built a giant wall to protect China. To the north was a big desert called the Gobi Desert. Dangerous groups lived there and tried to attack. The Great Wall was built to keep them out and keep the people of China safe.

The Qin Dynasty at a Glance

Achievement How it Helped China
Unification Brought many fighting kingdoms together as one country.
Standard Writing Allowed people in different areas to communicate easily.

The Great Wall Protected the people from invaders coming from the north.
