The Big Four: Comparing Colonies

How Different Goals Created Different Worlds

Key Words

  • Cash Crop: A crop like tobacco or sugar that farmers grow to sell for money.
  • Missionary: A person sent to teach their religion to people in a new country.
  • Territory: An area of land that a ruler or country controls.

The Three Big Reasons

During the Age of Exploration, European countries were driven by three powerful motivations known as the 3 G's: Gold, Glory, and God. Gold represented the hunt for riches like spices, gold, and silver. Glory was the desire to expand their empires and find fame for their country. Finally, God represented the goal of spreading religions like Catholicism or Protestantism to the Americas. Each country balanced these goals differently depending on what they hoped to find. Explorers had three main reasons for traveling. We call them the 3 G's. Gold was the search for money and riches. Glory was the search for fame and power for their country. God was the desire to spread their religion. Every country wanted these things, but they all had different plans.

Spain: The Conquistadors

Spain was the first major power to build a massive empire in the Americas. They were driven by the search for silver and gold mines. To control their new territory, they built missions where missionaries taught Christianity. Their culture left a lasting mark on the language and architecture of the American Southwest and Florida. Spain was the first country to stay in the Americas. They wanted to find gold and silver. They also built churches called missions to teach their religion. Today, many people in these areas still speak Spanish and live in towns built by Spain.

France: The Voyageurs

Unlike Spain, the French were not looking to build huge cities. Instead, they wanted beavers! French traders, known as Voyageurs, traveled deep into the forests of Canada. Because they needed the help of Native Americans to find and trap animals, the French formed strong alliances and often lived peacefully alongside Indigenous tribes. The French did not want to build big cities. They wanted beaver fur to sell in Europe. Because they needed help trapping animals, they became friends and partners with Native Americans.

The Netherlands: The Merchants

The Dutch were the ultimate businessmen. They set up "New Netherland" (New York) as a global trading hub. They were very tolerant of different religions because they believed that if you were a good worker, your faith didn't matter. Their economy was based almost entirely on the fur trade and shipping goods across the ocean. The Dutch were great at business. They built stores and ports in New York. They let people from many different religions live together. They just wanted to make sure everyone worked hard to trade and sell goods.
