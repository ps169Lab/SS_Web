Unit 4: Worlds Collide

The Age of Exploration and the Shaping of the Americas

The Big Idea

For thousands of years, the "Old World" and the "New World" lived in total isolation from one another. In this unit, we will explore the moment these two worlds finally met. We will see how the search for Gold, God, and Glory led to a global exchange of food, animals, and people that forever changed the environment, the economy, and the lives of millions. For a long time, people in Europe and America did not know each other existed. This unit is about the moment they finally met. We will learn why explorers traveled across the ocean and how their arrival changed the world's food, animals, and people forever.

Why are we learning this?

  • To understand our world: Almost every food you eat and the language you speak is a result of this "Great Collision."
  • To see multiple perspectives: We will learn how these events felt to European explorers, enslaved Africans, and Indigenous nations.
  • To solve problems: By looking at how cultures interacted in the past, we can learn how to build a more fair and connected world today.

How will I know I've learned it?

By the end of this unit, you will be able to complete the "Great Colony Comparison Project," where you will use evidence from multiple sources to explain which European power had the biggest impact on the Americas.
