New York Rights Movement

Learning About the Fight for Freedom and Equality in New York State

Welcome to Our Learning Journey

In this website, you will learn about brave people who fought for freedom and equal rights in New York State during the 1800s. You will discover how different groups of people did not have equal rights and freedoms, but how they worked together to bring about change.

What You Will Learn About

  • Slavery in New York: What life was like for enslaved people and how slavery ended in our state
  • Heroes of Freedom: Brave people like Harriet Tubman who helped others escape slavery
  • Women's Rights: How women fought for the right to vote and be treated equally
  • Working Together: How different groups joined forces to create a more fair society

Important Themes to Remember

As you read through these stories, remember these big ideas:

  • Different groups of people did not have equal rights and freedoms
  • People worked to bring about change
  • The struggle for rights and freedoms was one factor in the division of the United States that resulted in the Civil War

How to Use This Website

Click on the links in the menu above to read about different topics. Each page has vocabulary words to help you understand important terms. Take your time reading each story and think about how these events connect to each other.

These stories happened long ago, but they teach us important lessons about fairness, courage, and working together to make the world a better place for everyone.
