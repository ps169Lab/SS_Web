Crossing the Atlantic

Two Months at Sea: The Journey to New York

Expert Vocabulary

  • Hardtack: A very hard, dry biscuit that sailors ate because it didn't rot quickly.
  • Hull: The main body or "basement" of a ship where supplies and people stayed.
  • Scurvy: A sickness caused by not eating enough fresh fruits or vegetables.

A Floating Neighborhood

Imagine spending two months inside a wooden box that never stops moving. That was life on a ship like the Half Moon. Ships were packed with sailors, soldiers, and families looking for a new life. There were no private bedrooms; most people slept in hammocks or on the hard wooden floor in the hull. It was dark, damp, and very crowded. Because the ships carried animals like cows and chickens for food, it was also very noisy and smelly! Life on a ship was very hard. People lived in a wooden ship for two months. There were no real beds. Most people slept on the floor or in hammocks. It was crowded and dark. Ships also carried animals like cows and pigs. It was very loud and smelly inside!


Salt, Hardtack, and Survival

Since there were no refrigerators, food had to be preserved in salt. Sailors ate salted beef and a rock-hard cracker called hardtack. Hardtack was so tough that sailors often had to soak it in water or coffee just to take a bite! Sometimes, tiny bugs called weevils would crawl inside the crackers. Fresh water was also a problem; after a few weeks at sea, the water in the wooden barrels would turn green and slimy. This poor diet often led to a disease called scurvy. There were no refrigerators on the ships. Food was kept in salt so it would not rot. People ate a very hard cracker called hardtack. It was so hard they had to dip it in water to eat it. Sometimes bugs got into the food. The water on the ship often tasted bad and turned green.


Storms and Sea Monsters

The Atlantic Ocean is famous for its massive storms. When the waves grew high, the navigator would order everyone below deck to keep them from being washed overboard. Many children on these ships had never seen the ocean before and were terrified of the giant waves and the legends of sea monsters. However, the hope of finding "Glory" or starting a new home kept them going until they finally saw the coastline of New York. The ocean has big storms. When the waves were high, everyone had to stay inside the ship. Many people were scared. They had never seen the ocean before. They were afraid of storms and sea monsters. But they stayed brave because they wanted a new home in New York.


Class Discussion Questions

  • The Suitcase Challenge: If you were moving to New York on a ship and could only bring one small trunk, what 3 items would you pack to help you survive the two-month trip?
  • Hardtack vs. Pizza: Why do you think sailors were willing to eat "buggy" crackers and salty meat for months? Does this change how you think about the "Glory" they were searching for?
