The American Revolution in New York State

The Battle for the Heart of a New Nation

Welcome to the Revolution!

Imagine a time when the streets of Manhattan were filled with British soldiers, secret messages were hidden in laundry lines on Long Island, and the fate of a whole continent was decided in the woods near Saratoga. For seven long years during the American Revolution, New York State was the most important "square" on the chessboard of war.

The Strategic Prize

Why did so many battles happen here? It all comes down to **Geography**. The British believed that if they could control the Hudson River, they could "cut off" the colonies like a pair of scissors, separating New England from the rest of the country.


Because of our deep-water harbor and the long Hudson River, New York was the prize that both sides wanted most. From the fortresses in the Highlands to the busy docks of Manhattan, every corner of our state played a role in the struggle for liberty. Both sides wanted to control New York because of the Hudson River. They knew that whoever controlled the water could win the war. This made New York the most important place to fight.

Humanizing the History

History isn't just about dates; it's about the people. You will meet brave soldiers, clever spies like Anna Smith Strong, and leaders like Alexander Hamilton who called New York home.


As you move through these pages, you'll be acting as a historian. You'll look for evidence, decode secrets, and see how the choices made by New Yorkers over 250 years ago still affect the way we live in our state today. You will learn about the real people who lived through the war. Some were brave soldiers, and some were secret spies! Their choices helped make New York what it is today.
