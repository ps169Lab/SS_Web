The Spark of War

The French and Indian War: New York on the Front Lines

Expert Vocabulary

  • Territory: An area of land that a country claims to own and control.
  • Ally: A person or group that joins with another to help win a fight or a war.
  • Debt: Money that is owed because it was borrowed and must be paid back.

A Fight for the Map

Before the American Revolution began, another massive conflict called the French and Indian War (1754–1763) changed North America forever. It was a struggle between Great Britain and France to see who would control the land. Because New York sat right between the French in Canada and the British in New York City, our state became the most important battleground in the war. Before the Revolution, there was a big war in New York. It was called the French and Indian War. The British and the French both wanted to own the same land. Because New York was in the middle, most of the fighting happened here.

Choosing Sides in New York

The war involved more than just European soldiers. Native American nations had to make difficult choices about which side would best protect their homes. In New York, the powerful Iroquois Confederacy chose to be allies with the British. Meanwhile, other groups like the Algonquian and Huron tribes fought alongside the French. This war divided the people of New York as they fought for their territory. Native American groups had to pick a side in the war. In New York, the Iroquois tribes helped the British. Other tribes helped the French. They all fought hard to protect their homes and their land.

A Costly Victory

By 1763, the British won the war and took control of almost all the land in North America. However, the victory came with a huge problem: debt. The King of England had spent a fortune on ships, weapons, and soldiers. To pay back the money, the British government decided that the colonists in New York should start paying higher taxes. For the first time, the King began to keep a close eye on everything the colonists did. The British won the war! They took over the land. But the war cost a lot of money. The King was now in debt. He decided the colonists should pay him back with tax money. The King started making new, strict rules for New York.
