NEW YORK CITY GOVERNMENT

VOCABULARY

Mayor: The elected leader of a city who runs the city government.

Borough: One of the five major divisions of New York City (Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island).

Budget: A plan for how to spend money.

Public Advocate: A city official who helps citizens with complaints and watches over city agencies.

Term limits: Rules that limit how long an elected official can serve in office.

INTRODUCTION

New York City is the largest city in the United States, with over 8 million people living in its five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island. A city this big needs a special government to help it run smoothly. New York City has what's called a "mayor-council" form of government.


THE MAYOR: NEW YORK CITY'S LEADER

WHO IS THE MAYOR?

The mayor is the chief executive of New York City. This means the mayor is the top leader who runs the city government. The mayor is elected by the people of New York City and serves for four years. After four years, there is another election, and the mayor can run again.


WHAT DOES THE MAYOR DO?

The mayor has many important jobs:

  • Proposes the city's budget (plan for how to spend money)
  • Appoints (chooses) the heads of city agencies
  • Makes sure city services like police, fire, sanitation, and schools are working well
  • Represents the city when meeting with other government officials
  • Signs or vetoes (rejects) laws passed by the City Council

The mayor works at City Hall, a beautiful building in lower Manhattan.


THE CITY COUNCIL: NEW YORK CITY'S LAWMAKERS

WHAT IS THE CITY COUNCIL?

The City Council is New York City's legislative body. This means they make the laws for the city. The City Council has 51 members, each representing a different district (area) of the city.

WHAT DOES THE CITY COUNCIL DO?

The City Council has several important jobs:

  • Makes laws for the city
  • Approves the city's budget
  • Monitors city agencies to make sure they're doing their jobs well
  • Holds public hearings where citizens can share their opinions
  • Can override the mayor's veto with a two-thirds vote.

OTHER IMPORTANT CITY OFFICIALS

THE PUBLIC ADVOCATE

The Public Advocate is like a watchdog for the people. This person:

  • Helps citizens with complaints about city services
  • Reviews and investigates problems in city agencies

THE COMPTROLLER

The Comptroller is like the city's chief financial officer. This person:

  • Audits (checks) city agencies to make sure they're spending money properly

BOROUGH PRESIDENTS

Each of New York City's five boroughs has a Borough President. These officials:

  • Advocate for their borough's needs
  • Recommend projects for their borough

HOW CITIZENS CAN PARTICIPATE

Even if you're too young to vote, there are ways to participate in city government:

  • Attend Community Board meetings
  • Write letters to your City Council member
  • Visit City Hall
  • Learn about issues affecting your neighborhood
  • Volunteer for community projects

New York City's government works best when citizens are informed and involved!
