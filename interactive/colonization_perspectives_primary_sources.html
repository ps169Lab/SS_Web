Voices from the Age of Colonization

When Europeans arrived in the Americas, it was a world-changing moment — but it was experienced very differently depending on who you were. Read real words from people who lived through it. Each voice tells a different part of the same story.

These quotes come from real historical sources. Some have been lightly simplified so they are easier to read, but the meaning and the person who said them are real.

Below are quotes from three different perspectives. Notice how the same events — exploration, land, and trade — feel very different depending on who is speaking. Click any quote to read it in full.

The European perspective

European explorers and colonizers wrote about their voyages, the lands they found, and the people they met. Their writings often show excitement and ambition — but also attitudes about power and ownership that caused great harm. Reading their words helps us understand how they thought, even when we disagree with them.

The Native American perspective

Native American voices from this period are harder to find because European colonizers controlled most of the writing. But some words were recorded — by Native leaders speaking directly to colonizers, in oral traditions written down later, and by a few Native people who learned to write in European languages. These voices show strength, wisdom, and the deep pain of losing land and loved ones.

The African perspective

Millions of Africans were kidnapped and enslaved during the colonial period. Very few of their words were written down — because enslaving people often meant taking away their ability to read and write. The voices we do have, like Olaudah Equiano, are extraordinary and brave. Reading their words is one of the most important things we can do to understand what really happened.
