The Iroquois League

One of the most important developments in the Eastern Woodlands was the Iroquois League, also called the Haudenosaunee. This was a union of five tribes (later six) who agreed to work together instead of fighting each other. The tribes were the Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca, with the Tuscarora joining later. The Iroquois League was very important. Five tribes (later six) agreed to work together instead of fighting. These were the Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, Seneca, and later the Tuscarora.


The Iroquois League had a government where representatives from each tribe met to make decisions that affected all the tribes. This was one of the first democratic governments in North America, and it influenced the founders of the United States when they created the American government.
