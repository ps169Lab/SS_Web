The Fight for Freedom

Militias, Alliances, and the Road to Victory

Expert Vocabulary

  • Militia: A group of regular citizens (not professional soldiers) who organize to fight.
  • Alliance: When two or more countries agree to work together as a team.
  • Turning Point: A specific event that changes the direction of a war or story.
  • Surrender: When one side in a fight gives up and stops the battle.

Farmers vs. Professional Soldiers

When the war began at Lexington and Concord, the Americans didn't have a formal army. Instead, they relied on militias. These were farmers, shopkeepers, and blacksmiths who grabbed their muskets when the alarm rang. They didn't have fancy uniforms or perfect training like the British "Redcoats," but they knew the woods and hills perfectly. They used "hit-and-run" tactics, hiding behind stone walls and trees to surprise the British. The American militia were not regular soldiers. They were farmers and workers. They did not have uniforms. They used what they knew about the land to fight. They would hide behind trees and rocks to surprise the British army.

New York and the Turning Point

The British wanted to capture New York to split the colonies in half. If they controlled the Hudson River, they could stop the Patriots from sending help to each other. However, at the Battle of Saratoga in New York, the Americans won a huge victory. This was the turning point of the war. Because the Americans won, France decided to join the fight. This alliance with France brought more ships, more money, and more professional soldiers to help the Americans. The British wanted to take over New York. They wanted to stop the colonies from helping each other. But the Americans won a big battle at Saratoga. This changed everything! After this win, France decided to help the Americans. France sent ships and more soldiers to help win the war.

The Trap at Yorktown

By 1781, the British army was tired. They moved to Yorktown, Virginia, near the ocean, to wait for their ships. But the French navy arrived first! They blocked the water while the American and French armies surrounded the British on land. General Cornwallis was trapped. With no way to escape and no food coming in, the British had to surrender. This ended the major fighting of the war. The British army got trapped in Yorktown. The French navy blocked the ocean. The American army blocked the land. The British had no way to get out. They had to surrender and stop fighting. The war was finally over!

Independence is Official

Two years after Yorktown, both sides signed the Treaty of Paris. This paper officially recognized the United States as a free and independent nation. The British soldiers sailed away, and the colonies became states. Through bravery and the help of their French allies, the Americans had done the impossible: they had defeated the most powerful empire in the world. The leaders signed a paper called the Treaty of Paris. It said that America was now its own country. The British soldiers went home. The Americans won their freedom because they worked together and stayed brave.
