Moving West

The Big Idea and Big Changes of Manifest Destiny

Expert Vocabulary

  • Manifest Destiny: The 1800s belief that the U.S. was meant to spread from the Atlantic to the Pacific.
  • Annexation: When a country officially adds a new piece of land to its territory.
  • Homestead: A piece of free land given to settlers if they lived on it and farmed it.
  • Reservation: Land set aside by the government for Native American groups to live on.

The Big Idea: Manifest Destiny

In the 1800s, a powerful idea called Manifest Destiny took hold of the American imagination. Many people believed it was their special mission to spread their government and way of life across the entire continent. Imagine looking at a map and feeling like every mountain, forest, and river all the way to the Pacific Ocean was waiting for you! This belief gave settlers the courage to pack their lives into wooden wagons and head into the unknown, even though the journey was dusty, long, and dangerous. Long ago, many Americans believed in Manifest Destiny. This was the idea that the United States should own all the land from one ocean to the other. They thought it was their job to move West and build new towns. This idea made people want to travel a long way in wagons to find new homes.

Buying and Winning Land

The U.S. grew through big deals and wars. In 1803, the Louisiana Purchase doubled the size of the country overnight when France sold a massive piece of land for $15 million. Later, the U.S. added Texas through annexation. This led to the Mexican-American War. When the U.S. won, they gained even more territory, including California and the Southwest. To get people to move there, the government passed the Homestead Act, promising 160 acres of free land to anyone brave enough to farm the wild prairie for five years. The U.S. got more land in different ways. First, they bought a huge piece of land from France. Then, they added Texas. After a war with Mexico, the U.S. got even more land, like California. The government told people they could have free land if they moved West and started a farm. This was called a homestead.

The Cost of Expansion

While expansion meant new homes for some, it was a tragedy for others. For the Native American tribes who had lived on the land for thousands of years, Manifest Destiny meant the loss of their homes. Settlers fenced off hunting grounds and plowed over sacred sites. The U.S. government forced many tribes to leave their ancestral lands and move to reservations. This changed their way of life forever. The "trail" West was a place of hope for settlers, but a place of great sadness and injustice for the Indigenous people who were pushed aside. Moving West was very hard for Native Americans. They already lived on the land. Settlers took their hunting woods and their farms. The government made many Native Americans move to new areas called reservations. This was not fair to them. They lost their homes and their old way of life.

Class Discussion Questions

  • The Empty Land? Many settlers thought the West was "empty" land waiting to be used. Looking at the history of Native Americans, was it really empty? Why do you think settlers chose to see it that way?
  • The Free Land Deal: If someone offered your family 160 acres of land for free, but you had to build your own house and grow all your own food for five years, would you do it? What would be the hardest part?
