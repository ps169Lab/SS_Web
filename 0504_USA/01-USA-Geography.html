One Nation, Many Parts

Exploring the Four Regions of the United States

Expert Vocabulary

  • Region: An area of land that has common features like weather, history, or landforms.
  • Climate: The type of weather a place has over a long period of time.
  • Landscape: The visible features of an area, such as mountains, plains, or coastlines.

The Northeast and The South

The Northeast is known for its rocky coastlines, colorful autumn leaves, and bustling cities like New York. It has four distinct seasons, with freezing snowy winters and warm summers. Moving down the coast, The South feels very different. It is famous for its humid heat and long growing seasons. Instead of rocky cliffs, you find sandy beaches and wide, slow-moving rivers. The air often smells like damp earth and blooming flowers. The Northeast has big cities and cold, snowy winters. It has rocky land near the ocean. The South is much warmer. It has long summers and lots of rain. The land is flatter with many sandy beaches and big rivers.

The Midwest: The Heartland

In the middle of the country lies The Midwest. This region is mostly flat land called plains. Because the soil is so rich and deep, it is often called "America’s Breadbasket." You can stand in a field and see the horizon for miles in every direction. The weather can be extreme, with very hot summers and howling wind during the winter. It is also home to the Great Lakes, which look like giant blue oceans in the middle of the land. The Midwest is in the middle of the country. The land is very flat. It has great soil for farming corn and wheat. There are also huge lakes called the Great Lakes. The summers are hot and the winters are very windy.

The West: Mountains and Deserts

The West is the largest and most diverse region. It contains the jagged, snow-capped peaks of the Rocky Mountains and the dry, red sands of the desert. On the Pacific coast, you can see massive redwood trees that touch the clouds. Because it is so big, the weather changes quickly. You might find a rainforest in Washington state and a scorching desert in Arizona. It is a place of giant spaces and amazing natural wonders. The West is a very big area. It has tall mountains with snow on top. It also has dry deserts with red sand. Near the ocean, there are giant trees. Some parts are very wet, and other parts are very dry.
