Cardinal Directions

Learning North, South, East, and West

Vocabulary Words

  • Cardinal Directions - the four main directions: North, South, East, West
  • Compass - a tool that always points North
  • Navigate - to find your way from one place to another

What are Cardinal Directions?

Cardinal directions are important tools that help us understand and find our way around our world. There are FOUR Cardinal Directions: North (which means up), South (down), East (right), and West (left). They are very important for reading maps, exploring new places, and finding our way around. 

North is considered the "boss" of all directions, where the North Pole and Arctic are located. South is directly opposite North, where you find the South Pole. East is the direction where the sun rises in the morning. West is the direction where the sun sets in the evening. 

North and South are opposite each other, and East and West are also opposite. Besides the four main cardinal directions, there are also in-between directions: Northeast, Northwest, Southeast, and Southwest.

How are Cardinal Directions Used?

Cardinal directions are used in many ways to help us figure out where we are and find our way:

  • Reading Maps: They help us understand where things are located on a map. Maps can show us places like our school, city, or the whole world.
  • Using a Compass: A compass is a very important tool that always points North, which helps us figure out which way we are going.
  • Finding Your Way: Knowing cardinal directions is helpful when using tools like GPS. GPS uses satellites to find out where you are and can help you get from one place to another without getting lost.
  • Giving Directions: They are used in everyday life to give and follow directions, for example, when driving.
  • Exploring Places: Cardinal directions help us explore new or unfamiliar places. We can use tools like maps, globes, and compasses to imagine exploring new places.

How do Cardinal Directions Help Us?

Understanding cardinal directions provides us with a "secret superpower" for navigating the world. They help us find our way around, whether we are exploring a new neighborhood or planning a route in a large amusement park.

They help us find exact locations and understand where things are compared to each other. For example, you can use them to describe if a place is north, south, east, or west of where you are or a special landmark.

Knowing directions can help you plan the best route, such as to ride all the fun rides at an amusement park. They help us understand the world better by giving us a way to describe where places are and how they relate to each other.

Remember the Directions

A helpful tip to remember the order of cardinal directions is the mnemonic "Never Eat Soggy Waffles" or "Never Eat Slimy Worms," which stands for North, East, South, West.
