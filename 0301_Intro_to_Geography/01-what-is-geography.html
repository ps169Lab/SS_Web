What is Geography?

Learning About Our Amazing Planet Earth

Vocabulary Words

  • Geography - the study of Earth and how people live on it
  • Environment - the natural world around us
  • Continent - a very large area of land
  • Culture - the way people live, eat, and celebrate

Geography is Like Being a Detective

Geography is when we study our planet and how we interact with it. It's like being a detective, finding clues about the Earth, or having a superpower to uncover the world's secrets. The term "geography" itself comes from Greek words meaning "to draw, describe, or write about the earth".

How Geography Helps Us

Studying geography informs us about our planet and the world we live in. It helps us learn about different places like continents, countries, oceans, rivers, deserts, and mountains. We're going to learn what all of those words mean. Geography also explains why weather is different in different places and how weather or 'climate' and the other things in a certain place, make each place on Earth special and unique!

Geography also teaches us about the diverse ways people around the world eat, dress, and play. This year, we'll learn about different languages, traditions, and challenges people face in different parts of the world. We'll learn about natural environments, landforms, and how the Earth is always changing through processes like volcanoes, earthquakes, and shifting landscapes.

Tools that Help Geographers

When Geographers act like detectives to discover clues about our world, they use certain tools to help them Maps are drawings that show where things are in the world, like a picture from high above. There are different kinds of maps including physical maps that show natural features like mountains and rivers, and political maps that show the boundaries of countries and cities.

Other important tools include globes, which are round models of the Earth, and compasses that help us find our way by always pointing North. GPS uses satellites in space to help us find out where we are and get from one place to another without getting lost.

The Five Themes of Geography

Geography is organized around five big ideas that help us understand our planet:

  • Location: Where something is on Earth
  • Place: What a location is like, including its features and people
  • Human-Environment Interaction: How people change the world around them
  • Movement: How people, things, and ideas travel from place to place
  • Region: An area that has something in common, like climate or language

By engaging with these concepts and tools, anyone can become an expert navigator and gain a deeper appreciation for our world's wonders. Geography helps us become more knowledgeable about the world and its workings, which helps us address issues like pollution, natural disasters, and conflicts.
