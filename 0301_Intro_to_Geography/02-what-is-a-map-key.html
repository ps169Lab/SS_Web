What is a Map Key?

Understanding How to Read Maps Using Symbols

Vocabulary Words

  • Map Key - a box that explains what symbols on a map mean
  • Symbol - a picture that stands for something else
  • Legend - another name for a map key

What is a Map Key?

A map key is an important tool for understanding and reading maps. A map key is a little box of information usually found at the bottom of a map. It works just like a door key, because it unlocks all the information on the map so you can read and understand it.

The main job of a map key is to explain what the different lines and symbols on the map mean. A symbol is a picture that stands for something else on the map.

What Does a Map Key Look Like?

A map key looks like a box in the corner of the map. Inside this box, you'll find a list of symbols or small pictures, each with a word or explanation next to it, showing what that symbol means.Â 

Examples of Map Symbols

Examples of symbols you might find in a map key include:

  • Mounds of dirt representing mountains
  • Trees symbolizing forests
  • A small house image to denote a group of houses
  • A wavy line or blue area for water bodies like lakes or rivers
  • Dots of varying sizes to indicate cities, with a star sometimes used for important cities like a capital city

On special maps, such as a zoo map, symbols can show specific places like wheelchair paths, restrooms, picnic areas, information booths, or first aid stations. Other map keys might show symbols for forests, hills, roads, lookout points, castles, churches, or water birds.

What is a Map Key Used For?

The main use of a map key is to help you understand a map. Without it, it would be very hard to understand what the map is showing you. When you see a symbol on a map, you look at the map key to find that symbol and read what it means.

This helps you find natural features like mountains and rivers, locate buildings or roads that people made, understand different types of areas like parks or forests, and find important places when planning a trip or exploring a new place.

The map key is like a translator for the pictures on the map, making it possible to read and explore the world around us.
