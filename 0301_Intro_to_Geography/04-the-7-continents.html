What is a Continent?

A continent is defined as a very large area of land. On a globe, continents are among the easiest features to identify. Our planet Earth is divided into seven continents. They are larger than individual countries and contain various types of land and water. A continent is a very big area of land. On a globe, continents are easy to see. Earth has seven continents. They are bigger than countries.

The seven continents, listed from largest to smallest, are: Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, and Australia. Asia

Asia is the largest continent, covering one-third of all the land on Earth. It is home to a huge number of the world's people - two-thirds of everyone lives there in 48 countries. Asia includes China and India, which have the most people of any countries in the world. It has the world's highest mountain, the Himalayas, and the highest flat area, the Tibetan Plateau. It is surrounded by the Pacific Ocean, the Indian Ocean, and the Arctic Ocean. Asia is the biggest continent. Most of the world's people live there in 48 countries. China and India have the most people. Asia has the world's highest mountains and is surrounded by three oceans.


Africa

Africa is the second largest continent, located between the Atlantic and Indian Oceans. It has 54 different countries, which is more than any other continent. Africa is home to the world's longest river, the Nile, in the east, and the world's largest desert, the Sahara, in the northern part. It is known for its great variety of plants and animals, including many wild animals like lions, elephants, zebras, and rhinos. It tends to be very warm. Africa is the second biggest continent. It has 54 countries - more than any other continent. Africa has the longest river (the Nile) and the biggest desert (the Sahara). It has many wild animals like lions, elephants, zebras, and rhinos. Africa is very warm.


North America

North America is the world's third largest continent. It touches the Arctic Ocean to the north, the Atlantic Ocean to the east, the Caribbean Sea to the south, and the North Pacific Ocean to the west. It has 23 different countries, including the USA, Canada, Mexico, and the Caribbean islands. North America has natural wonders such as Yellowstone National Park and the Grand Canyon. The Panama Canal, which people built, separates it from South America. North America is the third biggest continent. It has 23 countries, including the USA, Canada, and Mexico. North America has amazing places like Yellowstone Park and the Grand Canyon.


South America

South America is the world's fourth largest continent. It touches the Pacific Ocean to the west and the Atlantic Ocean to the north and east. North America and the Caribbean Sea are to its northwest. It has 12 independent countries, some of which are famous for their soccer teams, like Brazil, Colombia, and Argentina. South America is home to the Amazon River rainforest, the world's driest desert (Atacama), and the world's longest mountain range (the Andes). It has unique animals such as capybara, llamas, and anacondas. South America is the fourth biggest continent. It has 12 countries, including Brazil, Colombia, and Argentina. South America has the Amazon rainforest, the driest desert, and the longest mountains. It has special animals like llamas and anacondas.


Antarctica

Antarctica is the fifth largest continent, located at the South Pole and is almost completely covered in ice. It has very cold temperatures, which can drop way below freezing. Even though it's so cold, it is home to different types of animals such as seals and sea birds. No people live there permanently, though scientists visit to study. The ice here makes the area even colder by bouncing the sun's light and heat back into space. It is an icy continent with very little plant and animal life. Antarctica is the fifth biggest continent. It's at the South Pole and covered in ice. It's very, very cold. Some animals like seals live there, but no people live there all the time. Only scientists visit to study.


Europe

Europe is the second smallest continent. It has nearly 51 independent countries with many different groups of people and languages. Its western coast touches the Atlantic Ocean, and it is separated from Africa by the Mediterranean Sea in the south. The European part of Russia is larger than any other country in Europe. Vatican City is the smallest country not only in Europe but also in the world. It lies across the Atlantic Ocean from North America and is known for its many old castles and stories about knights. Europe is the second smallest continent. It has about 51 countries with many different people and languages. Russia is the biggest country in Europe. Vatican City is the smallest country in the world. Europe has many old castles.


Australia

Australia is special because it is both a country and a continent. When New Zealand, New Guinea, and other islands are included, it is also called Australasia or Oceania. It sits between the Indian Ocean to the west and the Pacific Ocean to the east. Famous places include the Opera House in Sydney, the Great Barrier Reef in Queensland, and the Great Ocean Road in Victoria. It is the only continent that has just one country.
