Latitude and Longitude

Using Invisible Lines to Find Any Place on Earth

Vocabulary Words

  • Latitude - lines that go around Earth from east to west
  • Longitude - lines that go from North Pole to South Pole
  • Coordinates - the special address for any place on Earth
  • GPS - system that uses satellites to find locations

What are Latitude and Longitude?

Latitude and longitude are important imaginary lines that make a special address system on Earth, helping us find any location on the planet. Think of them as invisible lines that create a giant grid system covering the entire Earth.

Understanding Latitude Lines

Latitude lines, also called parallels, are circular lines that go around the Earth from east to west. They measure how far north or south a place is from the Equator.

The Equator is an imaginary line that goes around the middle of the Earth, dividing it into the Northern Hemisphere and the Southern Hemisphere. Latitude numbers go from 0° at the Equator to 90° North at the North Pole and 90° South at the South Pole.

Other important lines include the Tropic of Cancer, Tropic of Capricorn, Arctic Circle, and Antarctic Circle.

Understanding Longitude Lines

Longitude lines, also called meridians, are imaginary half-circle lines that run from the North Pole to the South Pole. They measure how far east or west a place is from the Prime Meridian.

The Prime Meridian is the 0° longitude line and passes near Greenwich, London. It divides the Earth into the Eastern Hemisphere and the Western Hemisphere. Longitude numbers go from 0° at the Prime Meridian to 180° eastward and 180° westward.

How They Work Together

When latitude and longitude lines are combined, they create a grid system on the Earth's surface, where each spot has its own special "address". This address is called coordinates, and it allows us to find any exact place on Earth.

How They Help Geographers

Geographers are like detectives for the Earth, discovering information about our planet. Latitude and longitude are very important tools in their work. They help geographers find exact locations on Earth and figure out where any place is.

They are very important for making and understanding maps and globes, which are important tools for studying the Earth's surface, features, continents, and countries. Geographers use these coordinates to study how different things are spread across the globe.

How They Help Us Every Day

Understanding latitude and longitude provides a "secret superpower" for navigating the world. They help us find our way around and locate any spot on Earth.

Modern tools for finding your way, like GPS on our cell phones, use latitude and longitude coordinates to help us find out where we are and get from one place to another without getting lost. They are very important for understanding time zones around the world, because longitude is connected to time.

Knowing these ideas helps us read maps for many reasons, whether reading a city map or exploring new or unfamiliar places. They give us a way to understand weather and cultures around the world, helping us learn about different parts of the world.
